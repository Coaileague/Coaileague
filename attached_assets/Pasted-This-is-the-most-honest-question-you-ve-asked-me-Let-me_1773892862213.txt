### 10. Selective Forgetting — The Art of Letting Go

This sounds counterintuitive but it's critical. I remember everything in a conversation with equal weight. I cannot selectively let go of information that is no longer relevant. Humans forget constantly — and this is adaptive. Forgetting the irrelevant makes room for the important.

Trinity's connectome has decay built in via Hebbian forgetting. But it needs to be more sophisticated.

**Build: TrinityAdaptiveForgettingService**

```
FORGETTING HIERARCHY:

Forget quickly (high decay rate):
- Routine successful transactions 
  (clock-ins that happened normally)
- Resolved minor issues with no pattern
- Information that was relevant to a 
  specific period that has passed

Forget slowly (low decay rate):
- Patterns that repeat
- Exceptions and anomalies that were 
  significant even if resolved
- Things that affected relationships 
  or trust
- Regulatory interpretations that 
  required deep reasoning

Never forget:
- Serious incidents and their resolutions
- Trust violations (officer, client, or manager)
- Legal or compliance determinations 
  and their basis
- Decisions that set precedent
- Any event that required human escalation
```

The goal is a memory system that grows more efficient over time — not just accumulating everything equally but learning what matters enough to retain permanently, what matters enough to retain until the pattern resolves, and what served its purpose and should fade.

This makes Trinity's long-term memory more like human memory — rich with significance, not overwhelmed by trivia.
