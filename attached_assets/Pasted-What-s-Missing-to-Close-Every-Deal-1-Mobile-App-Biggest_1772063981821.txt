What's Missing to Close Every Deal
1. Mobile App (Biggest Gap)
Security guards don't work at desks. Without a native iOS/Android app, no field-level adoption. Needs: GPS clock-in, guard tour QR/NFC scans, incident reports, shift acceptance, panic button, offline mode.

2. Live Field Map / Command Center
Dispatchers need a real-time map showing every officer's location, which post they're assigned to, and whether they're on-time or missing. This is a table-stakes feature for mid-size and large security companies.

3. Incident Reporting
Guards need to submit incident reports from the field — with photos, witness names, narrative, and supervisor sign-off. These feed into client reports automatically.

4. Background Check Live Integration
The Checkr/Sterling hooks exist but aren't wired to a real API. Companies won't sign without verified background check automation. This includes continuous monitoring (re-screening annually), not just onboarding.

5. Certification & License Renewal Automation
Auto-alerts at 60/30/7 days before guard card, CPR, firearms permit, or first aid cert expiry. Blocks scheduling if a cert is expired. This alone is a major pain point operators deal with manually today.

6. Panic Button / SOS Alert
Guards in the field need a one-tap emergency alert that immediately notifies dispatch, supervisors, and 911 context. This is a liability and safety requirement for most enterprise clients.

7. Geofencing + Post Departure Alerts
Automated alerts when a guard leaves a post boundary unexpectedly. Clients demand this for high-security sites.

8. Client Billing Automation (End-to-End)
Right now billing is manual review. Auto-generate client invoices from approved timesheets → send to QuickBooks → trigger Stripe payment → mark paid. Full cycle without human touch.

9. Shift Coverage Marketplace (Public Board)
When a shift goes unfilled, post it to a pool of pre-vetted on-call officers across the region who can claim it. This solves the #1 operational pain — last-minute no-shows.

10. Union & Seniority Rules Engine
Many large security companies are unionized. Scheduling must respect seniority for shift assignment, overtime rules, and grievance periods. Without this, enterprise contracts are impossible.

11. Daily Activity Reports (DAR)
Each officer submits a structured end-of-shift report — activity summary, incidents, equipment checks, visitor log. Clients receive these automatically as branded PDFs.

12. Anonymous Ethics Hotline
Officers can anonymously report misconduct, safety violations, or harassment. These route to HR with Trinity AI triage. Required for enterprise compliance programs.

13. ADP/Paychex Direct Payroll Push
Most mid-to-large security companies already use ADP or Paychex. CoAIleague needs a native sync that pushes approved timesheets directly to payroll — no CSV exports.

14. RFP Response Automation (Claude)
Security staffing contracts start with RFPs. Trinity / Claude can auto-draft compliant proposals from the client's posted requirements, pulling org certifications, insurance info, and officer qualifications automatically.

15. Client SLA Monitoring
Contractual obligations: response time, minimum coverage hours, supervisor inspection frequency. Auto-flag SLA breaches before the client notices them.
