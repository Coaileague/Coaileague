## CATEGORY 1: THE NEW HIRE PIPELINE
### From "I Need Someone" to "Licensed Officer on Schedule"

This is one of the most painful operational workflows in the security industry
and CoAIleague has almost none of it built. Right now the platform assumes
officers already exist and are already licensed. The real world doesn't work that way.

### 1A — RECRUITING TRIGGER

```
WHEN TRINITY DETECTS:
  - Officer headcount falls below org's minimum bench threshold
  - A site is consistently hard to fill (3+ consecutive weeks of open shifts)
  - An officer gives notice or is terminated
  - A new client contract is won and requires additional officers

TRINITY SHOULD:
  - Alert owner: "You need [n] additional [level] officers for [site/region]"
  - Generate a job posting draft based on the position requirements
  - Post to integrated job boards if connected (Indeed, ZipRecruiter, etc.)
  - Track applicants in a pipeline with status
```

### 1B — APPLICANT TRACKING

```
Trinity needs an applicant pipeline:
  Applied → Screened → Background Initiated → Licensed → Onboarding → Active

Trinity actions needed:
  hiring.create_posting
  hiring.track_applicant
  hiring.initiate_background_check  ← integrate Checkr or Sterling
  hiring.update_applicant_status
  hiring.generate_offer_letter
  hiring.send_offer
```

### 1C — BACKGROUND CHECK INTEGRATION

```
Security industry requires criminal background checks.
Most states require them before licensing.
Texas DPS requires criminal history for PERC.

Integration needed: Checkr, Sterling Talent Solutions, or Accurate Background

Trinity actions:
  background.initiate_check(applicantId, checkType)
  background.get_status(checkId)
  background.review_results(checkId)
  background.flag_disqualifying_finding(checkId)

Trinity intelligence:
  - Know which findings are disqualifying per state
  - Know which findings require individualized assessment
  - Know FCRA adverse action requirements before rejecting
```

### 1D — LICENSING & TRAINING PIPELINE MANAGEMENT

```
This is the biggest gap in the entire platform.
A new hire in Texas cannot work until they have a PERC card.
Getting a PERC card requires:
  1. Criminal background check (DPS)
  2. 6-hour pre-assignment training (Level II)
  3. DPS application and fee
  4. Fingerprinting
  5. DPS processing (2-6 weeks)

Trinity needs to manage this entire pipeline:

  onboarding.initiate_perc_application(employeeId)
  onboarding.schedule_pre_assignment_training(employeeId)
  onboarding.track_dps_application(employeeId)
  onboarding.confirm_license_received(employeeId)
  onboarding.mark_schedule_eligible(employeeId)

Trinity proactive behavior:
  - Tracks each new hire's pipeline stage
  - Alerts when application is taking longer than expected
  - Estimates when officer will be schedule-eligible
  - Flags if training slot is needed
```

### 1E — ELECTRONIC ONBOARDING DOCUMENTS

```
Every new hire needs:
  - I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification (federal requirement)
  - W-4 Federal Tax Withholding
  - State tax withholding form
  - Texas New Hire Reporting (state requirement — within 20 days of hire)
  - Direct deposit authorization
  - Emergency contact information
  - Uniform acknowledgment
  - Policy handbook acknowledgment
  - Post order acknowledgment (site-specific)
  - Non-disclosure agreement (if applicable)

Trinity actions:
  onboarding.send_document_packet(employeeId)
  onboarding.track_completion(employeeId)
  onboarding.flag_missing_documents(employeeId)
  onboarding.submit_new_hire_report_to_state(employeeId)  ← legal requirement
  onboarding.confirm_i9_complete(employeeId)
```

## CATEGORY 2: POST ORDERS — THE MOST CRITICAL MISSING DOCUMENT

Post orders are the legal document that governs officer behavior at each site.
They define exactly what an officer is authorized to do at that specific location.
They are the most important document in the security industry from a liability perspective.
CoAIleague apparently has no post orders module.

This is a significant platform gap.

### 2A — WHAT POST ORDERS MUST CONTAIN

```
Per site, per client, Trinity should be able to generate and manage:

SITE INFORMATION:
  - Property address and contact
  - Client contact and emergency contacts
  - Site hours of operation
  - Access control procedures

OFFICER DUTIES:
  - Specific patrol routes and timing
  - Access control procedures
  - Visitor management procedures
  - Package/delivery handling
  - Key control procedures

AUTHORITY AND LIMITATIONS:
  - What the officer IS authorized to do at this site
  - What the officer is NOT authorized to do
  - Detention authority (has the client authorized shopkeeper's privilege?)
  - Trespass authority (is the officer authorized to give criminal trespass warnings?)
  - Weapons authorization (armed? what type?)

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES:
  - Fire/evacuation procedure
  - Medical emergency procedure
  - Active threat procedure
  - Natural disaster procedure
  - Who to call in each scenario

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS:
  - What must be reported to client
  - What must be reported to company
  - Report format and timing

PROHIBITED ITEMS/BEHAVIORS:
  - What is not allowed on this property
  - How to handle violations
```

### 2B — TRINITY'S POST ORDERS CAPABILITY

```
Trinity actions needed:
  postorders.generate_from_client_profile(clientId, siteId)
  postorders.assign_to_officer(officerId, siteId)
  postorders.confirm_officer_read_and_acknowledged(officerId, siteId)
  postorders.get_for_shift(shiftId)   ← Trinity reads these before answering site questions
  postorders.flag_deviation(incidentId, postOrderSection)
  postorders.update_section(siteId, section, content)
  postorders.version_history(siteId)  ← track changes, who signed what version

Trinity intelligence:
  When an officer asks "what am I supposed to do if someone refuses to leave?"
  Trinity pulls the post orders for their current assignment and answers from those,
  not from generic security knowledge. The answer for Pinnacle Tower may differ
  from Heritage National Bank.
```

## CATEGORY 4: COMPENSATION INTELLIGENCE BEYOND BASIC PAY

### 4A — SHIFT DIFFERENTIALS

```
Most security companies pay more for:
  - Night shifts (after 10pm or 11pm)
  - Weekend shifts
  - Holiday shifts
  - Hazardous duty assignments
  - Armed vs unarmed premiums (some pay this as a differential, not a separate rate)
  - Supervisor differential (not always a different base rate — sometimes an add-on)

Trinity needs to:
  - Read shift differential rules from org settings
  - Apply them automatically at payroll calculation
  - Show the breakdown on pay stubs
  - Factor differentials into billing rates (if armed differential = cost increase, bill rate must cover it)

compensaton.get_differential_rules(orgId)
compensation.apply_differentials(timesheetEntry)
compensation.calculate_blended_rate(employeeId, period)
```

### 4B — ON-CALL COMPENSATION

```
Officers on the on-call pool may have a right to on-call pay in some states.
TX doesn't require it but some org policies provide it.

Trinity should:
  - Track who is designated on-call each week
  - Apply on-call compensation per org policy
  - Track and compensate if on-call officer is actually called in
```

### 4C — MILEAGE AND EXPENSE TRACKING

```
Officers who travel between multiple sites in a day may be entitled to mileage reimbursement.
(IRS 2026 rate applies)

Trinity actions:
  expense.submit_mileage(officerId, date, fromSite, toSite, miles)
  expense.submit_expense(officerId, type, amount, receipt)
  expense.approve_expense(expenseId, managerId)
  expense.include_in_payroll(expenseId)
  expense.generate_expense_report(orgId, period)
```

### 4D — GARNISHMENTS AND INVOLUNTARY DEDUCTIONS

```
When an officer has a court-ordered garnishment (child support, tax levy, bankruptcy),
the employer is legally required to withhold and remit it.
Failure is a federal/state violation.

Trinity actions:
  payroll.add_garnishment(employeeId, garnishmentDetails)
  payroll.calculate_garnishment(employeeId, grossPay)  ← must respect disposable income limits
  payroll.remit_garnishment(garnishmentId, payPeriod)
  payroll.generate_garnishment_report(period)

Trinity intelligence:
  - Know federal Consumer Credit Protection Act limits on garnishments
  - Know Texas exemptions (TX is very protective of employees from wage garnishment)
  - Flag if garnishment would reduce pay below minimum wage
  - Know priority order when multiple garnishments exist (child support first)
```

## CATEGORY 5: OFFICER SAFETY — THE LONE WORKER PROBLEM

Security officers often work alone. Nights. Remote locations. High-risk environments.
This is a significant liability gap and a genuine safety issue.

### 5A — WELFARE CHECK SYSTEM

```
Trinity monitors officer safety during active shifts:

  WELFARE CHECK PROTOCOL:
  - At configurable intervals (every 1 hour, every 2 hours) during solo shifts
  - Trinity sends officer a check-in prompt: "Check-in required — respond OK"
  - Officer responds via app
  - If no response in [configurable window — 15 minutes]:
    → Trinity attempts second contact
    → If still no response → alerts supervisor immediately
    → Supervisor attempts contact
    → If supervisor can't reach: → contact emergency services at site location

Trinity actions:
  safety.initiate_welfare_check(officerId, shiftId)
  safety.receive_checkin_response(officerId)
  safety.escalate_missed_checkin(officerId, shiftId, missedCount)
  safety.log_safety_event(officerId, type, notes)

This feature alone could be the reason a client chooses CoAIleague.
It directly addresses officer safety liability.
```

### 5B — PANIC BUTTON / DURESS ALERT

```
Officer in danger presses emergency button in the app.

Trinity receives duress alert and simultaneously:
  1. Notifies supervisor with GPS location
  2. Notifies client emergency contact
  3. Initiates 911 call guidance for nearest responder
  4. Begins recording audio if phone permits and officer has enabled
  5. Logs everything with timestamps for incident record

safety.receive_panic_alert(officerId, location, timestamp)
safety.initiate_emergency_protocol(officerId)
safety.notify_emergency_chain(officerId, location)
```

### 5C — VEHICLE PATROL TRACKING

```
Officers conducting vehicle patrols need route tracking.
Trinity should:
  - Track GPS route during patrol
  - Flag if officer deviates significantly from patrol route
  - Log patrol completion for client reporting
  - Detect if vehicle is stationary too long during patrol (officer may be in distress)
```

```
Most security companies don't actually know which clients make them money.
Trinity should calculate and surface:

  Per client:
  - Total revenue billed
  - Total labor cost (pay + burden)
  - Gross profit and margin %
  - Change over time (is this client getting less profitable?)
  - Cost of turnover at this site (onboarding cost of replacement officers)

  - Revenue per hour
  - Labor cost per hour
  - Net margin per hour
  - OT cost impact on margin

  - Net value contribution
  - OT frequency impact on cost

analytics.site_profitability(siteId, period)
analytics.officer_value_analysis(officerId, period)
analytics.margin_trend(orgId, periods)
```

```
  - Suggest optimal invoice timing to minimize cash gap

finance.payroll_coverage_check(orgId, nextPayDate)
```
