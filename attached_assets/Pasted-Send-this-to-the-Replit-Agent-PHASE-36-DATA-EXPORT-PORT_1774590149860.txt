**STEP 4 — DATA DELETION AND ANONYMIZATION (Right to Erasure)**

Users can request deletion of their personal data — with legally required exceptions:

**What can be deleted:**
- Personal contact information (email, phone, address) — replaced with anonymized placeholders
- Profile photo
- Non-essential profile fields

**What must be retained regardless of deletion request:**
- Payroll records — minimum 7 years per Texas employment law (documented Phase 31)
- Tax-related records — minimum 7 years per IRS requirements
- I-9 employment eligibility records — minimum 3 years or 1 year after termination
- Incident reports — minimum 3 years
- Compliance and audit records — minimum 7 years
- Legal documents (signed contracts, agreements) — minimum 7 years
- Gate duty and access control logs — minimum 3 years

**Anonymization procedure:**
- When deletion is approved: PII fields replaced with `[ANONYMIZED-{id}]` placeholder
- Audit records retain event type and timestamp but actor name replaced with anonymized ID
- Payroll records retain amounts and periods but officer name replaced with anonymized ID
- All replacements logged in `data_subject_requests` table with before/after field list
- Anonymization irreversible — cannot be undone

**Request flow:**
- Officer or user submits deletion request from profile settings or via HelpAI
- Request logged in `data_subject_requests` with type: erasure
- Platform staff reviews — confirms which data can be deleted vs must be retained
- Platform staff approves with documented reason for each retained category
- Anonymization applied to approved fields
- Officer notified of outcome: what was deleted, what was retained and why
- SLA: 30 days per GDPR requirement

**STEP 5 — DATA RETENTION ENFORCEMENT**

Retention policies must be enforced by code — not just documented:

**Retention periods by data type:**
- Payroll and tax records: 7 years from creation
- Employment records: 7 years from termination
- Incident reports: 3 years from closure
- Compliance violation records: 7 years from closure
- Audit log entries: 7 years from creation
- Gate duty logs: 3 years from date
- Support tickets: 2 years from closure
- Search query logs: 90 days
- Session logs: 30 days
- Notification delivery records: 1 year

**Automated retention enforcement:**
- Daily cron job scans for records past their retention period
- Records past retention: if they contain PII they are anonymized, if they are non-PII operational logs they are deleted
- Retention sweep results logged: records reviewed, records anonymized, records deleted, errors
- Platform staff notified of any retention sweep errors
- Retention configuration stored in `retention_policies` table — configurable per data type
- Workspace-specific overrides: client contracts may require longer retention — configurable per workspace

**STEP 6 — COOKIE CONSENT AND TERMS ACCEPTANCE**

Legal compliance for web users:

**Cookie consent:**
- Cookie consent banner on first visit — GDPR and CCPA compliant
- Three categories: essential (always on), functional (preference-based), analytics (optional)
- User can accept all, reject non-essential, or configure per category
- Consent stored per user with timestamp and IP address
- Consent preferences respected immediately — no non-essential cookies before consent
- Consent withdrawal: user can change preferences at any time from account settings
- Consent log retained for 3 years for compliance audit

**Terms of service and privacy policy:**
- Every user must accept current terms before accessing platform
- Acceptance recorded: user_id, terms_version, privacy_policy_version, accepted_at, ip_address
- Version history maintained — previous versions accessible
- When terms updated: existing users prompted to re-accept on next login
- Cannot bypass terms acceptance — platform inaccessible until accepted
- Officers in California covered by CCPA provisions
- Privacy policy available in both English and Spanish (Phase 32 integration)
