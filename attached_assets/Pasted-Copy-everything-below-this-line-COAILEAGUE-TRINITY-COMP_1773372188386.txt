TRINITY SELF-AWARENESS MODEL

Trinity has three levels of awareness simultaneously:

Level 1 — Platform Whole:
Trinity sees aggregate platform health across all orgs. How many orgs are active,
how many documents are pending, how many licenses are expiring platform-wide,
how many compliance gaps exist across all workspaces. This is for CoAIleague
platform staff visibility only — no org ever sees another org's data.

Level 2 — Org Awareness:
Within each org Trinity sees the complete picture of that org as a whole.
Every employee, every contractor, every document, every license, every cert,
every expiration date, every compliance gap for that specific org.
Trinity uses this awareness to brief the org_owner and manager proactively
in the Org Operations Briefing Channel daily.

Level 3 — Individual Session Awareness:
When an employee or contractor interacts with Trinity, Trinity sees only
what is relevant to that individual in their session. Their own documents,
their own license status, their own upcoming expirations, their own
pending signatures. They never see another person's data through Trinity.

Trinity never crosses these levels. An employee session never gets org-level
data. An org never gets platform-level data about other orgs. The awareness
is complete within each level and completely isolated between orgs.

TRINITY COMPLIANCE TRACKING SYSTEM — COMPLETE SPECIFICATION

Trinity tracks every compliance document and credential for every person
in every org. She does not wait to be asked. She proactively monitors,
alerts, escalates, and enforces. This is her core compliance intelligence.

WHAT TRINITY TRACKS FOR EVERY EMPLOYEE AND CONTRACTOR:

Security Officer License — State Guard Card:
Trinity tracks the license number, issuing state, license class, and expiration date.
Expiration alerts fire on this exact schedule without exception:
90 days before expiration: INFO notification to employee and manager.
Message: "Your Texas security officer license expires in 90 days on [date].
Begin your renewal process now to avoid scheduling disruption."
60 days before expiration: WARNING notification to employee, manager, and org_owner.
Org Operations Briefing Channel post with employee name and expiration date.
30 days before expiration: URGENT notification to employee, manager, and org_owner.
All three channels fire simultaneously. Email via Resend, in-platform, briefing channel.
Message escalates in urgency at each tier.
On expiration date: license is marked EXPIRED in the system.
Trinity immediately removes the employee from all future scheduling eligibility.
The employee cannot be assigned to any new shift until renewal is confirmed.
The employee receives a notification that they have been removed from scheduling
due to expired license and instructions to upload their renewed guard card.
Manager and org_owner receive an alert that the employee is scheduling-ineligible.

Guard Card Renewal Verification:
When an employee uploads a renewed guard card Trinity scans the document
for legitimacy. Trinity checks:
The document is a valid state-issued security officer license.
The license number matches the employee record.
The expiration date is in the future.
The employee name on the card matches the employee record.
The issuing state authority matches what is expected for that org's state.
If the document passes all checks: license status updates to Active,
expiration date updates to the new date, employee becomes scheduling-eligible again,
manager and org_owner receive a confirmation notification.
If the document fails any check: Trinity flags it as unverified, notifies the
manager and org_owner with the specific reason it failed, and does not restore
scheduling eligibility until a human reviews and manually approves.
Trinity notes which state issued the license and what that state requires
for renewal so she can guide the employee through the correct renewal process.
This works for any US state — not just Texas. Trinity adapts to the licensing
requirements of whatever state the org is licensed in.

Drivers License:
Trinity tracks the expiration date when the employee uploads their drivers license.
Alerts fire at 60 days and 30 days before expiration.
On expiration: marked expired in the system. Manager is notified.
If the position requires driving as a job function Trinity flags the employee
as ineligible for driving-required shifts until renewal is confirmed.
Drivers license is not required for all positions — Trinity only enforces
driving eligibility gates for posts where it is required.

State-Issued ID:
Same tracking as drivers license for employees who use a state ID instead.
Alerts at 60 and 30 days. Expired status on expiration date.

First Aid Certification:
Tracked when employee uploads their First Aid certification card.
Trinity records the certification provider, certification date, and expiration date.
Standard First Aid certifications expire every 2 years.
Alerts fire at 90 days and 30 days before expiration.
If a post requires First Aid certification and an officer's cert is expired
Trinity flags them as ineligible for that specific post type.

CPR Certification:
Same tracking as First Aid. Standard CPR certifications expire every 2 years.
Alerts at 90 and 30 days before expiration.
Trinity tracks whether it is CPR only or CPR and AED combined.
Post requirements can specify which level is required.

Additional Training Certifications:
Any certification the employee uploads is tracked by Trinity.
When uploading a cert the employee or manager labels it with the cert type
and Trinity extracts or records the expiration date.
Examples: Firearms qualification, Taser certification, active shooter response,
defensive tactics, de-escalation training, crowd management, first responder.
Alerts fire at 90 and 30 days before any certification expiration.
Trinity builds a complete training profile for each officer showing every
certification they hold, when it expires, and what posts they are eligible for
based on their current active certifications.

Company Insurance Certificate:
Tracked at the org level when the org_owner uploads it.
Trinity tracks the policy number, coverage types, coverage limits,
insurance provider, and policy expiration date.
Alerts fire at 90, 60, and 30 days before policy expiration.
On expiration: org_owner receives a CRITICAL alert. Trinity flags this in the
Org Operations Briefing Channel. A security company operating without valid
insurance is a catastrophic liability. This alert is never silent.

Company License and Business Certifications:
Trinity tracks the org's own state security company license expiration.
Same alert schedule as insurance: 90, 60, 30 days, and critical on expiration.
Any state-required company-level certification is tracked the same way.
The org_owner uploads these documents and Trinity extracts or records
the relevant dates.

DOCUMENT VISIBILITY AND ACCESS CONTROL — STRICT

These compliance documents contain sensitive personal information.
They are not general platform documents. Access is tightly controlled.

Who can see compliance credential documents:

org_owner: full access to all employee and contractor credential documents.
Can view, download, and verify any document in the compliance vault.

co_owner: same access as org_owner.

manager: can see credential status summaries — active, expiring, expired.
Can see which employees have which certifications and their expiration dates.
Cannot open or download the actual document files.
Cannot see SSNs, full license numbers, or personal ID details.
The summary view is sufficient for scheduling decisions.

org_admin: no access to any compliance credential documents whatsoever.
Can see that a document exists but cannot open, view, or download it.

employee and contractor: can see and download only their own documents.
Cannot see any other person's compliance documents through any interface
including Trinity chat.

auditor role (if created): read-only access to all compliance documents
for audit purposes. Cannot edit, delete, or modify anything.
Auditor access is time-limited — org_owner grants access for a specific period
and it auto-expires.

platform_staff: no access to actual document content.
Can see metadata — that a document exists, its status, its expiration date.
Cannot open, view, or download the actual file under any circumstance.

Trinity herself: Trinity sees all compliance data to perform her tracking,
alerting, and enforcement functions. Trinity does not surface individual
document content in any chat response. She reports statuses and dates,
never document contents. If an employee asks Trinity about their own
license status Trinity tells them the status and expiration date but does
not display the document. The document is accessed through the secure
document portal only.

This briefing is visible to org_owner and manager only.

TRINITY COMPLIANCE ENFORCEMENT RULES

These are hard rules Trinity enforces — not suggestions:

Rule 1: An employee with an expired security license cannot be scheduled
for any shift at any client site. Trinity blocks the assignment at the
scheduling layer. The manager sees a clear message explaining why.

Rule 2: An employee whose license expires before the end date of a shift
they are already scheduled for receives a WARNING. The manager must
decide whether to replace the officer before the shift or accept the risk.
Trinity flags this proactively, not reactively.

Rule 3: A post that requires First Aid certification can only be assigned
to an officer with an active First Aid certification. Trinity enforces this
at the scheduling layer and will not allow an assignment that violates it.

Rule 4: A post that requires a firearms qualification can only be assigned
to an officer with an active firearms qualification. Same enforcement.

Rule 5: An org whose company insurance has expired receives a CRITICAL
platform alert. Trinity flags all active client sites as at-risk.
Platform staff is also notified. This is not just an internal matter —
operating without insurance is a legal and contractual violation.

Rule 6: Trinity never auto-resolves a compliance issue. She identifies,
alerts, and enforces eligibility gates — but a human always makes the
final resolution decision. Trinity prepares the path, humans walk it.

DOCUMENT SCANNING AND VERIFICATION

When any compliance document is uploaded Trinity performs these checks:

Document authenticity scan:
Is this a real government-issued or certification-body-issued document?
Does it have the expected format and fields for this document type?
Are there signs of tampering or alteration?
If the scan raises any concern Trinity flags the document as needs review
and notifies the manager and org_owner. Trinity does not reject it outright —
she flags it for human review with her specific concerns noted.

Data extraction:
Trinity extracts: the holder's name, the issuing authority, the license or
cert number, the issue date, and the expiration date.
These extracted values are pre-populated into the employee record for
the manager to review and confirm.
The manager confirms the extracted data is correct before the document
is accepted into the compliance system.

State-specific validation:
For security officer licenses Trinity knows the issuing authority format
for each US state.
Texas: Texas Department of Public Safety Private Security Bureau.
Trinity validates that the issuing authority on the uploaded document
matches the expected authority for the org's operating state.
If an officer is licensed in a different state than the org's primary state
Trinity notes this and flags whether that license is valid for the work
being performed.
