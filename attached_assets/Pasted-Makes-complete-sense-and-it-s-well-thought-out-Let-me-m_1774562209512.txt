**CoAIleague Per-Seat Pricing Logic**

The model is simple: every active officer on the platform is a billable seat at $25/month. The tier determines how many seats are included in the base price and what features are unlocked. Going over the seat limit triggers per-seat overage billing automatically.

**Revised tier structure:**

| Tier | Base Price | Included Seats | Per-Seat Overage | Target Customer |
|------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Starter | $199/mo | 10 seats | $25/seat | Small independent operators |
| Professional | $749/mo | 100 seats | $25/seat | Companies like Statewide |
| Business | $2,249/mo | 300 seats | $25/seat | Regional security companies |
| Enterprise | $6,999/mo | 1,000 seats | $25/seat | Securitas, Allied, GuardWorld scale |
| Strategic | Custom | Custom | $25/seat | Enterprise partnerships, state contracts |

**The overhead math behind $25/seat:**

At $25 per active officer per month the platform covers: Replit hosting and compute, OpenAI/Claude/Gemini API calls (Trinity, HelpAI, voice, document parsing), Resend email delivery, SMS via Twilio, push notification delivery, QuickBooks API calls, document storage, and all other external services — while maintaining 50-90% gross margins as originally designed.

**Overage billing logic:**

When a workspace's active officer count exceeds their tier's included seat count — for example a Professional workspace at 100 seats adds officer #101 — the overage kicks in automatically. The extra officers are billed at $25/seat/month prorated from the date of activation. Stripe handles the proration calculation. The org_owner sees a real-time seat counter showing current/included and a projected overage charge before confirming activation.

**Hard cap vs soft cap:**

The included seats are a soft cap — you can go over, you just pay overage. The only hard cap scenario is if the org_owner has explicitly set a spending limit in their workspace settings. If they set a hard cap Replit stops activation at the limit and shows an upgrade prompt instead of allowing overage.
