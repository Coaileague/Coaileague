**Phase 33 — State Regulatory Auditor Partner Portal**

This is one of the most strategically important phases in the entire build. The goal is to make CoAIleague the platform that state regulatory bodies trust and partner with — starting with Texas DPS and TCOLE and expanding to all 57 states in the knowledge base. A state auditor who can conduct a full, verified, frictionless audit through CoAIleague is a state auditor who recommends CoAIleague to every security company they regulate.

**Architecture:**

State Regulatory Authority (SRA) accounts are a completely separate account type from workspace users. They are not org_owners, managers, or officers. They are verified external principals with time-bounded, scope-limited, read-only access to specific workspaces they are authorized to audit.

**Identity Verification Flow:**

When a state auditor requests access to audit a workspace, they submit: their full legal name, badge number, regulatory body (e.g. Texas DPS, FDLE), state, authorization letter (uploaded PDF), and government-issued ID. Trinity receives this package and runs verification: confirms the regulatory body is in the state knowledge base, validates the authorization letter format against known regulatory document templates, extracts the audit scope (which company, which period, which license types), flags any inconsistency for human review. Platform staff reviews Trinity's analysis and approves or denies the SRA account creation. On approval an SRA account is created with a unique credential set — username format `SRA-[StateCode]-[BadgeNumber]`, password generated and delivered via verified government email. Trinity logs the full identity verification chain with all submitted documents stored in a secure SRA vault.

**SRA Login and Session:**

SRA users log in through a dedicated portal at `/regulatory-audit` — completely separate from the main platform login. Two-factor authentication required on every login. Session is time-bounded to the audit period specified in the authorization letter. Session is workspace-scoped to the specific company being audited. SRA cannot navigate to any other workspace. SRA session logs every action: page viewed, document opened, data exported, chat message sent — with timestamp, IP address, and action type. Org_owner is notified via NDS the moment an SRA auditor logs in, including auditor identity and stated purpose.

**What SRA Can See — Read-Only:**

- Officer roster: names, license numbers, license types, license expiration dates, certification status, hire dates, post assignments — matching DPS audit requirements for that state
- License and certification records: full history, renewal dates, document vault entries
- Compliance records: violations, resolutions, compliance scores, audit reports
- Incident reports: all incidents within the audit period for the workspace
- Document vault: executed contracts, onboarding documents, compliance packets — filtered to audit-relevant types only
- Schedule history: post coverage records, shift logs, clock-in/out records for the audit period
- Training records: if applicable for the state's requirements
- Proof of service logs: GPS-stamped activity records

**What SRA Cannot See:**

- Payroll amounts, pay rates, or financial data beyond what is required for compliance
- Internal platform communications between staff
- Other workspaces — complete isolation
- System configuration or platform settings
- Other auditors' sessions or findings

**SRA Communication:**

SRA auditor can initiate a chat session with the workspace's org_owner and designated managers. Chat is mediated through the platform — not email. All messages logged immutably as part of the audit record. Org_owner can respond in real time or asynchronously. Trinity monitors the chat for requests that require document retrieval — if the auditor asks for a specific record, Trinity can surface it from the vault directly in the chat. SRA can submit formal findings and evidence requests through the platform — each request creates a structured record with: request type, description, urgency, requested documents, response deadline. Workspace receives NDS notification of each formal request.

**SRA Evidence Package:**

At any point during or after the audit, SRA can generate an evidence package: a structured export of all data they viewed during the audit, all documents they accessed, all communications, and their formal findings. This package is generated as a PDF with SHA-256 integrity hash. CoAIleague retains a copy of every evidence package in the SRA vault. Workspace receives a copy automatically.

**Multi-State Operation:**

Each SRA account is scoped to one state. A Texas DPS auditor cannot see California BSIS data. State-specific audit fields pulled from `stateRegulatoryKnowledgeBase` — each state's auditor sees exactly the fields their regulatory body requires, no more. State knowledge base defines what each regulatory body is authorized to view in CoAIleague. Future partnership agreements with state regulatory bodies formalized as state configurations in the knowledge base.

**Partnership Vision:**

The goal is for CoAIleague to become the official audit platform preferred by state regulatory bodies. When a regulator completes a frictionless, fully documented, AI-assisted audit through CoAIleague, they have a reason to recommend it. Trinity's role is to make the auditor's job easier — surfacing relevant records proactively, answering regulatory questions about the company's compliance history, and producing audit-ready exports in the exact format the regulatory body needs.
