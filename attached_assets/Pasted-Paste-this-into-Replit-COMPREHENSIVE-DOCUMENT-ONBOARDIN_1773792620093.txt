**SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS REFERENCE**

Before scanning, understand what must exist:

**Client data required for Trinity to orchestrate:**
- Company name, billing address, work location address (can differ)
- Primary contact name, phone, email
- Accounts payable contact name, phone, email (separate from primary)
- Service type requested — unarmed (Level 2), armed (Level 3), PPO (Level 4)
- Number of officers required per shift
- Days and hours of coverage needed
- Bill rate per hour per officer type
- Billing cycle — daily, weekly, biweekly, monthly
- Invoice delivery email
- Signed service proposal (required at some point, does not block shift creation)
- Signed general services contract (required for compliance, does not block operations)
- State ID of responsible party uploaded
- Client portal access credentials

**Employee/contractor documents required for compliance:**
- Online application (fillable, signable)
- Regulatory employee/contractor packet containing:
  - Full legal name, DOB, full place of birth, current address
  - Guard card number, issue date, expiry date, license type (Level 2/3/4)
  - State ID — color front and back uploaded
  - Social security card — front uploaded
  - Drug-free workplace acknowledgment — signed online
  - Employee handbook acknowledgment — signed online
  - Liability clause acknowledgment — signed online
  - Reference check — last worked location and contact
  - W4 (W2 employees) or W9 (1099 contractors)
  - I9 employment eligibility verification
  - Direct deposit authorization form
  - Post orders / SOP acknowledgment — assigned per client site, must be seen, acknowledged, and signed before first shift at that site
  - Uniform and equipment issuance record
- All documents compiled into a single chronological PDF upon onboarding completion
- All documents persistent, searchable, downloadable, printable until explicitly purged
- System reminders sent to employee AND org when any required document is missing before billing occurs

**PASS 5 — DOCUMENT SIGNING PIPELINE AUDIT**

Trace every document that requires a signature through the complete signing pipeline.

**For each signable document type — verify the full pipeline:**

Step 1 — TRIGGER: What initiates the signature request? Manager action? Trinity automation? Employee onboarding step?
Step 2 — FETCH: Is the document fetched with correct workspace and assignee context?
Step 3 — VALIDATE: Is the document in the correct state? Does the signer have a valid email?
Step 4 — PROCESS: Is the signature request prepared correctly with signer name, email, and document?
Step 5 — MUTATE: Is the e-signature provider API called? Is document status updated to `pending_signature`?
Step 6 — CONFIRM: Is the signature request confirmed? Is the signer notified?
Step 7 — NOTIFY: Is the org owner notified the request was sent? Is `document_signature_requested` published?

**Webhook completion pipeline:**

Step 1 — TRIGGER: Webhook fires from e-signature provider
Step 2 — FETCH: Is the local document fetched by provider document ID?
Step 3 — VALIDATE: Is webhook signature verified? Is document in `pending_signature` state?
Step 4 — PROCESS: Is the signed PDF downloaded from the provider?
Step 5 — MUTATE: Is document status updated to `signed`? Is `signedAt`, `signedByUserId`, `signerIpAddress` recorded?
Step 6 — CONFIRM: Is signed PDF stored and retrievable?
Step 7 — NOTIFY: Is org owner notified? Is `document_signed` published? Is onboarding status rechecked?

**Silent failure guards:**
- If provider API call fails — is document status rolled back?
- If webhook never fires — is there a polling fallback?
- If signed PDF download fails — is document status NOT updated to signed?
