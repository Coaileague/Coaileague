**How Extensions Work — The Technical Reality**

Extensions in a programmable phone system are not physical extensions like an office PBX. They are just logic — when someone presses 1 Trinity plays a different script and takes different actions. All of this is coded in CoAIleague, not in Twilio.

Twilio's job is simple:
- Answer the call
- Play audio or text-to-speech
- Listen for keypresses or speech
- Send what it hears to CoAIleague
- Execute whatever CoAIleague tells it to do next

CoAIleague's job is everything else:
- Decide what to say
- Understand what the caller said
- Take actions in the platform
- Tell Twilio what to do next

So yes — all extension logic is coded here in CoAIleague. Twilio is just the phone carrier. We own the entire experience.

---

**The Technical Flow**

```
Caller presses 2 (Client Services)
         ↓
Twilio sends to CoAIleague:
POST /api/voice/inbound/keypress
{ Digits: "2", CallSid: "CA...", workspaceId: "acme" }
         ↓
CoAIleague runs clientServicesHandler()
→ Generates TwiML response
→ Tells Twilio: "Say this, then listen for speech"
         ↓
Twilio speaks Trinity's words
→ Records caller response
→ Sends audio to CoAIleague
         ↓
CoAIleague sends audio to Whisper
→ Gets transcript
→ Trinity processes transcript
→ Generates next response
→ Returns TwiML to Twilio
         ↓
Loop continues until call ends
```

Everything is real-time back and forth between Twilio and CoAIleague. Latency is typically 1-2 seconds per exchange which feels natural in conversation.
