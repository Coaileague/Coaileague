THE CORE PHILOSOPHY — SOFT GATES AND HARD BLOCKS

Security workforce compliance is not binary. There is a spectrum.
Trinity must understand this spectrum and apply it correctly.

HARD BLOCKS — regulatory requirements that cannot be bypassed without violation:
Expired security officer license (guard card) — armed or unarmed.
This is a state regulatory requirement in every jurisdiction.
An officer with an expired guard card cannot legally perform security work.
Trinity must never allow scheduling of an expired-license officer.
If management manually overrides this hard block the system must:
Allow the override to proceed since management has the authority.
Record it immediately as a regulatory violation event.
Log every detail: who overrode, which officer, which shift, which site,
what client, what time, what the license expiration date was at time of override.
Generate a violation record that is immutable — WORM locked.
Notify the org_owner that a regulatory violation has been recorded.
Include this violation in the auditor-facing compliance report.

Other hard blocks that constitute regulatory violations:
Operating an armed officer without an active firearms qualification.
Assigning an officer to a post requiring a specific license class
they do not hold.
Any state-specific hard requirement confirmed by Trinity's regulatory knowledge base.

SOFT GATES — compliance factors that affect scheduling preference not eligibility:
Incomplete onboarding documents — officer is less favorable but not blocked
until their first scheduled shift date arrives.
Missing non-required certifications for posts that recommend but do not require them.
Low punctuality score — history of late clock-ins makes the officer less favorable.
Incomplete post orders acknowledgment for a site they have not worked before.
GPS clock-in failures — history of GPS issues at specific sites.
Disciplinary actions on file — active disciplinary record reduces scheduling priority.
Outstanding document signatures — not a hard block but reduces favorability.

OFFICER COMPLIANCE SCORE — TRINITY CALCULATES THIS:
Every officer has a compliance score from 0 to 100.
100 is fully compliant across all variables.
The score is calculated by Trinity in real time from actual database records.

Score components and weights:
License active and not expiring within 30 days: 40 points.
License expiring within 30 days: 20 points (reduced but not zero).
License expired: 0 points AND hard block applied.
Onboarding documents complete: 20 points.
Onboarding documents pending: 10 points.
Post orders signed for assigned sites: 15 points.
Post orders pending for assigned sites: 8 points.
No active disciplinary actions: 15 points.
Active disciplinary action: 8 points.
GPS clock-in reliability above 90 percent: 10 points.
GPS reliability below 90 percent: 5 points.

Score interpretation:
85 to 100: highly favorable — Trinity selects these officers first.
70 to 84: favorable — good candidate, minor gaps.
50 to 69: less favorable — used when better options unavailable.
30 to 49: low priority — used only when no better option exists.
0 to 29: minimum priority — Trinity flags to manager for attention.
0 with expired license: hard blocked regardless of score.

SCHEDULING ENGINE WITH COMPLIANCE SCORING:
Trinity ranks candidates by compliance score first, then availability,
site familiarity, overtime proximity, and certification match.
Trinity shows the manager the compliance score and reasoning next to every candidate.
Full transparency — Trinity never hides the compliance reasoning.

VIOLATION TRACKING SYSTEM

Every time a hard block is manually overridden a violation record is created.

VIOLATION RECORD SCHEMA:
violation_id, workspace_id, officer_id, override_by_user_id, violation_type,
shift_id, site_id, client_id, officer_license_number, license_expiration_date,
shift_date, shift_start_time, shift_end_time, override_reason (required),
override_timestamp, was_corrected, correction_timestamp,
state_license_authority, regulatory_reference (statute citation), created_at.
All violation records are WORM locked immediately on creation.
No violation record can ever be edited or deleted by anyone.

VIOLATION NOTIFICATION CHAIN:
In-platform notification to org_owner with full violation details.
Email to org_owner via Resend.
Entry in org regulatory compliance dashboard under Violations.

VIOLATION SUMMARY REPORT — REPORTBOT GENERATES THIS:
Generated automatically at end of each month, on demand, and when auditor logs in.
Contains: executive summary, violation detail table, compliance trend chart,
officer compliance score summary, Trinity AI narrative, regulatory citations,
corrective action log.
Saved to document safe under Regulatory Compliance. WORM locked.

AUDITOR PORTAL — FULL SPECIFICATION

AUDITOR PORTAL PAGE:
Public-facing at coaileague.com/regulatory or audit.coaileague.com.
Professional design clearly branded for regulatory use.
Completely separate from main platform login.

AUDITOR VERIFICATION FLOW — 6 STEPS:

Step 1 — Company lookup:
Auditor enters the security company's state license number.
Trinity searches for an org with that license number.
If found Trinity shows company name and state for confirmation.
If not found Trinity returns a clear not found message.

Step 2 — Auditor credentials submission:
Full name, state agency name, official agency email address,
auditor badge number or employee ID, purpose of audit
(routine, complaint-based, random, or renewal inspection),
upload of official audit authorization document — PDF of audit order or
state-issued authorization letter.

Step 3 — Trinity automated verification:
Does the email domain match the known regulatory agency domain for that state?
Trinity maintains a registry of all US state security regulatory agency email domains.
Does the uploaded authorization PDF contain:
Auditor name matching submitted name.
License number matching the company being audited.
State agency letterhead or seal.
Audit authorization date within the last 30 days.
If all checks pass: marked verified_pending.
If any check fails: specific reason returned with correction request.

Step 4 — Org_owner notification:
Trinity immediately notifies the org_owner via in-platform notification and email:
Agency name, auditor name, audit type, and that credentials were verified.
Org_owner has 24 hours to dispute. No dispute means access is granted automatically.
Disputes escalate to CoAIleague platform_staff for manual review.

Step 5 — Auditor account creation:
After 24 hours with no dispute access is granted.
Temporary login credentials sent to auditor's official state email address.
Account scoped to that specific org only — no other org visible.
Read-only — no edits, no deletions, no data changes possible under any circumstance.
Time-limited — expires at end of audit period (14 days default).

Step 6 — Audit completion:
Auditor uploads their official audit report PDF to the platform.
Trinity reads it, extracts findings, violations noted, corrective actions required,
compliance status determination, and required deadlines.
Trinity generates a corrective action plan for the org_owner.
Org_owner notified of audit report upload and corrective action plan.
Auditor account auto-deactivates on expiry.
Full access log permanently stored in audit trail.

Trinity analyzes the photo using vision AI to confirm:
SECURITY text is visible.
Company name is visible.
The uniform is not deceptively similar to a law enforcement uniform.

If any element is missing or unclear: Trinity flags the specific gap.
Upload slot with date recorded.
If not uploaded: flagged as MISSING — REQUIRED FOR AUDIT.

SECTION 5 — PATROL VEHICLE DOCUMENTATION (IF APPLICABLE):
