## THE DELEGATION & VERIFICATION LOOP — THIS IS PARTIALLY OPEN

We covered that Trinity can delegate. What we didn't fully close is **whether Trinity closes the loop on what she delegates.**

This is the difference between a manager who assigns work and a manager who manages.

```
WHAT'S MISSING:

task.create(assignedTo, dueBy, context)
  → Trinity creates a trackable task when she delegates anything

task.track_status(taskId)
  → Trinity checks back — not just once, on a schedule

task.escalate_overdue(taskId)
  → If supervisor hasn't approved timesheets in 24hrs → nudge
  → If nudge ignored for another 12hrs → escalate to owner
  → Trinity never delegates and forgets

task.verify_completion(taskId)
  → When task is marked done, Trinity validates the output
  → Payroll approved? Trinity checks the math before marking complete
  → Schedule published? Trinity scans it for conflicts before confirming
  → Invoice sent? Trinity confirms delivery receipt before closing

task.log_delegation_chain(taskId)
  → Full audit trail of who was assigned what, when, what happened
```

**The core principle:** Trinity doesn't just delegate and move on. She delegates, tracks, verifies the result meets the standard, and only closes the loop when it actually does. If the result doesn't meet the standard she flags it — not to punish but to catch errors before they become problems.
