### Layer 2 — Officer Performance Scoring System

This is the intelligence layer that makes Trinity's suggestions meaningful rather than random. Every officer has a living performance score Trinity maintains and updates continuously.

**Build: TrinityOfficerPerformanceEngine**

```
officer_performance_scores table:

- last_calculated_at
```

**Scoring weights (configurable per workspace):**

### Layer 5 — Disciplinary Pattern Analyzer

This is the most sensitive capability and requires the deepest thinking before any action. Trinity never accuses — she identifies patterns, investigates context, and presents evidence.

**Build: TrinityDisciplinaryAnalyzer**

Trinity monitors for:

```
PATTERN DETECTION:
Tardiness: 3+ late clock-ins in 30 days
  → Trinity investigates: what shifts, 
    what sites, what days of week, 
    is there a pattern (always Monday? 
    always same site? always after payday?)
  → Before flagging: is there a legitimate 
    explanation in the record?
  → Suggestion level: verbal reminder 
    through supervisor

Calloff pattern: 3+ calloffs in 60 days
  → Trinity investigates: are they always 
    on weekends? Always before holidays? 
    Is there a health pattern? 
    Did something change at the site?
  → Cross-reference: is this correlated with 
    anything (new client, new supervisor, 
    schedule change)?
  → Suggestion level: welfare check first, 
    then formal counseling if no explanation

Report delinquency: 2+ missed/late DARs
  → Trinity investigates: is the officer 
    working shifts that make reporting hard? 
    Are they on sites without good connectivity?
  → Suggestion: training first, then 
    progressive discipline

Client complaint: verified legitimate complaint
  → Trinity does full investigation:
    Who filed, what exactly happened, 
    is there incident record, what does 
    the DAR say, is this pattern or isolated?
  → Trinity's deep think before any action:
    5W1H analysis on the complaint
  → If first complaint, low severity: 
    supervisor counseling suggestion
  → If pattern of complaints: 
    site reassignment recommendation to manager
  → If serious single complaint: 
    owner escalation immediately

Dereliction: officer failed to perform 
  documented post duties
  → Trinity cross-references post orders 
    against DAR submissions and supervisor reports
  → Presents evidence to supervisor with 
    specific instances documented
```

**The deep think requirement before any disciplinary suggestion:**

Trinity must answer all 5Ws before surfacing any disciplinary suggestion:

- **Who** — confirmed identity, confirmed it's this officer not a data error
- **What** — exactly what policy was violated, what was the expected behavior
- **When** — specific dates and times, is this a pattern or isolated
- **Where** — which site, which shift, under whose supervision
- **Why** — has Trinity considered alternative explanations? Is there context that changes the interpretation?
- **How** — how should this be handled given severity, tenure, history, and policy

Only after all 5 answered does Trinity surface the suggestion. And even then it goes to the appropriate tier — never straight to owner unless critical.

```
ADJACENT SCOPE (Trinity engages briefly,

- Personal problems unrelated to work — 
  Trinity acknowledges with care and

OUT OF SCOPE (Trinity redirects clearly
```

Warm but professional. Like a genuinely caring supervisor who remembers your name, knows your history, and wants you to succeed — but maintains appropriate professional distance.

Not robotic: "Clock-in recorded at 22:00."

Right: "You're all set Marcus — clocked in at Lone Star Medical right on time. Have a good shift tonight."
