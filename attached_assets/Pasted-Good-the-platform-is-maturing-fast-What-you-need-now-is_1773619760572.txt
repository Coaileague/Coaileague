**PILLAR 1 — CLIENT LIFECYCLE**
- Can a new client be created with all required billing data — name, address, email, armed/unarmed rate, overtime rate, contract rate, billing cycle?
- Does the client record have a field for invoice delivery email separate from the contact email?
- When a client is created, does Trinity automatically initialize their billing profile and schedule a first invoice run?
- Can an invoice be generated, previewed, approved, sent to the client email, marked paid, and pushed to QuickBooks — without any manual steps outside the platform?
- Is there an automatic follow-up sequence when an invoice goes unpaid past due date?
- Is profit/loss per client calculable from existing data — revenue minus officer pay minus platform fees?

**PILLAR 2 — EMPLOYEE/CONTRACTOR LIFECYCLE**
- Can a new employee be created with all required data — name, address, SSN or EIN, pay rate, employment type (W2/1099), direct deposit account, DPS license number, expiry date, certifications, emergency contact?
- Is direct deposit account information stored securely with field-level encryption or equivalent?
- When payroll runs, does it generate a pay stub record per employee with gross pay, deductions, net pay, and pay period?
- Can an employee download their own pay stub from the platform?
- Is there a year-end tax document generation path — W2 for employees, 1099 for contractors?
- Are terminated employees' records preserved with a termination date and reason for tax and compliance records?

**PILLAR 3 — PAYROLL PIPELINE**
- Is every payroll run tied to approved timesheets only — can unapproved time entries enter payroll?
- Is there an approval chain: timesheet submitted → manager approved → payroll drafted → owner approved → processed?
- Does payroll push to QuickBooks automatically after owner approval?
- Is there a payroll lock — can the same pay period be processed twice?
- Are contractor payments handled separately from W2 payroll with correct 1099 tracking?

**PILLAR 4 — INVOICE PIPELINE**
- Is every invoice line item traceable to a specific shift, officer, and approved timesheet?
- Can the org owner see a full audit trail — shift worked → timesheet approved → invoice line item → invoice sent → payment received?
- Does the platform handle partial payments on invoices?
- Is there a collections escalation path — overdue → reminder → final notice → collections flag?
- Are invoice records preserved permanently for tax purposes even after client cancellation?

**PILLAR 5 — TRINITY DATA ACCESS**
- Does Trinity have read access to all client billing settings when generating invoices?
- Does Trinity have read access to all employee pay rates and direct deposit status when running payroll?
- When Trinity detects a missing required data field — no client email, no employee pay rate, no direct deposit account — does it surface a specific actionable alert to the org owner rather than silently failing?
- Can Trinity produce a profit/loss summary for any date range on demand?
- Can Trinity identify which clients are most profitable and which are operating at a loss?

**PILLAR 6 — RECORDS & TAX**
- Are all financial records — invoices, payroll runs, pay stubs, payments — write-protected after a close period?
- Is there an export function for all financial records by date range for tax filing purposes?
- Is there an audit log of every financial mutation — who changed what, when, and what it was before?
