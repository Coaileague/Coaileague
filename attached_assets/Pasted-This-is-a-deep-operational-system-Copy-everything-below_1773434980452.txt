STEP 2 — SHIFT ROOM FULL ARCHITECTURE SPEC

After the audit build everything missing according to this exact specification.

SHIFT ROOM CREATION:
Every time a shift is assigned to one or more officers a shift room is automatically created.
The room is created by the system not by any user.
Room name format: Shift — [Site Name] — [Date] — [Start Time to End Time]
Example: Shift — Lone Star Industrial Park — March 15 — 22:00 to 06:00
Room type: shift_room
Room is linked to the shift record by shift_id.
Room is linked to the workspace by workspace_id.
Room has a status: pre_shift, active, completed.
Room status changes to active when the shift start time arrives.
Room status changes to completed when end-of-shift report is generated and saved.
Room is read-only after completed status — no new messages can be posted.

WHO IS IN THE ROOM:
When the room is created these participants are automatically added:
ReportBot — always present in every shift room, cannot be removed.
HelpAI — always present in every shift room, cannot be removed.
The assigned officer or officers — added automatically on room creation.
The site manager or shift supervisor if one is assigned to the site.
The org_owner and co_owner have read access to all shift rooms but are not active participants.
Managers have read access to all shift rooms for their assigned sites.
Employees can only see shift rooms they are personally assigned to.
No employee can see another employee's shift room.

OFFICER ENTRY FROM CHAT DOCK:
When an officer has an active or upcoming shift a shift room card appears in their chat dock.
The card shows: site name, shift date and time, room status (pre_shift / active / completed).
The officer taps the card to enter the room.
On entry ReportBot sends a greeting message:
"Welcome to your shift room for [Site Name] on [Date]. I am ReportBot.
Use this room to post your hourly check-ins, site photos, and incident reports.
I will organize everything into a shift report at the end of your shift.
Post a photo with your location to begin your shift documentation."

OFFICER CHAT DOCK VISIBILITY RULES:
Officers see only their own shift rooms.
Officers see only shift rooms where their shift is today or within the next 24 hours.
Completed shift rooms remain visible for 7 days then archive.
Officers cannot create new rooms from the chat dock — they can only enter assigned rooms.
The chat dock always shows a Rooms tab and a Direct Messages tab.
The shift room card is the most prominent item in the Rooms tab when a shift is active.

STEP 3 — REPORTBOT FULL SPECIFICATION

ReportBot is an autonomous intelligence under Trinity orchestration.
She does not wait to be summoned. She is active in every shift room from creation to completion.
She has specific behaviors at specific times. Every behavior is automatic.

REPORTBOT ON SHIFT START:
When the shift start time arrives ReportBot sends the opening check-in prompt:
"Shift started. [Officer Name], please post your first check-in photo showing
your current position at [Site Name]. Include the time and confirm you are on post."

REPORTBOT ON HOURLY CHECK-IN:
Every hour during the shift ReportBot sends a check-in reminder if the officer
has not posted anything in the last 55 minutes:
"Hourly check-in reminder — please post a photo or update from your current position."
If the officer has been active in the last 55 minutes ReportBot does not send the reminder
to avoid interrupting active documentation.

REPORTBOT PHOTO ACKNOWLEDGMENT:
When an officer posts a photo ReportBot immediately acknowledges it:
ReportBot reads the image metadata — timestamp and GPS coordinates if available.
ReportBot cross-references the GPS coordinates against the site geofence.
If coordinates are within the geofence: "Photo logged at [Time]. Location confirmed at [Site Name]."
If coordinates are outside the geofence: "Photo logged at [Time]. Location shows outside
expected site boundary. Please confirm your position."
If no GPS metadata: "Photo logged at [Time]. GPS data not available for this image.
Please confirm your current location in a message."
Every photo is assigned a log entry number: Photo 1, Photo 2, and so on.
The log entry number is used in the final report.

REPORTBOT MESSAGE ACKNOWLEDGMENT:
When an officer posts a text message ReportBot acknowledges it:
For routine check-in messages: "Check-in noted at [Time]."
For incident reports: "Incident logged at [Time]. This will be included in your shift report.
Please include: what happened, who was involved, any actions taken, and whether police or
emergency services were contacted."
For anything that appears urgent based on content: ReportBot escalates to the manager
immediately with a notification: "ReportBot — Possible incident reported at [Site Name].
Officer [Name] posted: [message excerpt]. Please review immediately."
This escalation fires via in-platform notification and email to the shift manager.

REPORTBOT OFFICER IDENTITY VERIFICATION:
ReportBot verifies that the person posting in the room is the officer assigned to the shift.
Posts are tied to authenticated user sessions — a post can only come from a logged-in user.
If a post comes from a user who is not assigned to this shift room ReportBot flags it:
"Message received from an unassigned user. Only officers assigned to this shift
may post in this room. This event has been logged."
The org_owner is notified of any unauthorized post attempt.

REPORTBOT END OF SHIFT:
30 minutes before shift end ReportBot sends:
"Your shift ends in 30 minutes. Please post your final site status update
and any outstanding observations before shift end."

At shift end time ReportBot sends:
"Shift ended. Compiling your shift report now. This may take a moment."

ReportBot then compiles the complete shift report.

STEP 4 — SHIFT REPORT GENERATION SPECIFICATION

This is the most important ReportBot function.
At end of shift ReportBot compiles everything posted in the room into a
professional organized PDF report. The report format is fixed and consistent
every time regardless of how many posts were made.

REPORT STRUCTURE:

HEADER:
Security company name and license number (Track B — no CoAIleague branding visible to client).
Report title: SHIFT ACTIVITY REPORT
Site name and address.
Shift date.
Shift time: start time to end time.
Officer name and badge or license number.
Report ID: auto-generated unique identifier.
Report generated timestamp.

OFFICER AND ASSIGNMENT SUMMARY:
Officer full name.
Officer security license number and class.
Assigned post.
Site supervisor name and contact if applicable.
Relief officer name if applicable.

HOURLY LOG — CHRONOLOGICAL:
Every hour of the shift gets its own section.
Each section shows: Hour label (22:00 — 23:00), then all posts during that hour in time order.
For each photo: log number, time, GPS confirmation status, a thumbnail of the photo.
For each text post: time, officer name, full message text.
For each incident report: time, labeled as INCIDENT REPORT in red or bold,
full incident description, actions taken, whether emergency services were contacted.
If no activity was logged in a given hour: note reads "No activity logged this hour."

POST-PROCESSING:
Before the report is finalized ReportBot runs a quality scan:
Spelling check — flag any misspelled words and correct them in the report.
If a word is ambiguous ReportBot uses context to determine the correct spelling.
Articulation check — if a message is unclear or grammatically broken ReportBot
rewrites it in professional security report language while preserving the meaning.
The officer's original message is preserved in a footnote.
This ensures every shift report reads professionally regardless of how it was typed.
Completeness check — if any hour has no logged activity ReportBot flags it
with a note: "No officer activity documented for this period."
This creates accountability — gaps in documentation are visible in the report.

SIGNATURE SECTION:
Officer digital signature line (links to the officer's acknowledgment in the document pipeline).
Supervisor review signature line.
Date and time of report generation.
Statement: "This report was automatically compiled by ReportBot under Trinity AI orchestration
and reviewed for accuracy. Original message content is preserved in the CoAIleague audit trail."

FOOTER:
Powered by CoAIleague in subtle gray text.
Report ID for audit reference.
Page numbers.
Watermark: SHIFT REPORT across the body in light gray diagonal text.

PDF GENERATION AND STORAGE:
The report is generated as a professional PDF using the existing PDF generation service.
File name format: ShiftReport_[SiteName]_[Date]_[StartTime]_[ReportID].pdf
The PDF is saved to the org document safe under the Shifts category automatically.
The document safe entry includes: site name, officer name, shift date, shift time, report ID.
The report is WORM locked immediately on save — it cannot be edited or deleted.
The audit trail logs the report generation with timestamp and who it was generated by (ReportBot/system).

CLIENT DISTRIBUTION (OPTIONAL):
After the report is saved the manager or org_owner receives a notification:
"Shift report for [Site Name] on [Date] is ready. View or send to client."
The manager can review the report in the document safe.
The manager can choose to send the report to the client contact via a single button.
If sent the client receives a Track B branded email with the PDF attached.
Email subject: Shift Activity Report — [Site Name] — [Date]
The send action is logged in the audit trail.
Trinity is aware of whether reports are being sent to clients and includes
this in the weekly operational summary.

STEP 5 — HELPAI IN SHIFT ROOMS

HelpAI is present in every shift room as a resource for officers.
HelpAI does not interrupt unless addressed.
Officers can ask HelpAI questions at any time during their shift.

What HelpAI can do in a shift room:
Answer questions about post orders for this specific site.
Provide emergency contact numbers for this site.
Explain reporting procedures for specific incident types.
Tell the officer their clock-out time and overtime status.
Answer questions about company policy from the employee handbook.
Provide first aid guidance if an officer reports a medical situation.
Provide de-escalation language if an officer reports a confrontational situation.

HelpAI cannot do in a shift room:
Access financial data, billing, or payroll information.
Access any other officer's shift room or data.
Perform actions that require manager or org_owner authorization.

When an officer types @HelpAI or asks a question HelpAI responds.
HelpAI responses are clearly labeled with the HelpAI name and avatar.
ReportBot responses are clearly labeled with the ReportBot name and avatar.
Each bot has a distinct visual identity in the room so officers always know who they are talking to.

STEP 6 — MEETING ROOMS AND MEETINGBOT

When a meeting is created in the platform a meeting room is automatically created.
Room name format: Meeting — [Meeting Title] — [Date]
MeetingBot auto-enters the room on creation.
MeetingBot opening message: "Meeting room is open for [Meeting Title] on [Date] at [Time].
I will keep a record of this meeting and generate a summary when the meeting ends."

MeetingBot functions during the meeting:
Notes every message posted with a timestamp.
If someone posts @MeetingBot action item: [text] MeetingBot adds it to the action items list.
If someone posts @MeetingBot decision: [text] MeetingBot adds it to the decisions list.

MeetingBot at meeting end:
Generates a meeting summary with: attendees, duration, key points discussed,
decisions made, action items with owner names, next meeting date if mentioned.
Meeting summary is saved to the document safe under the Meetings category.
Every attendee receives the summary via in-platform notification.

STEP 7 — CLOCKBOT FORCED CLOCK-IN SYSTEM

ClockBot can be summoned by any manager or org_owner when an officer is
having trouble clocking in through the normal GPS clock-in flow.

CLOCKBOT SUMMONING:
Manager opens the officer's shift room or direct message.
Manager types @ClockBot or clicks a Clock In Officer button in the shift room header.
ClockBot appears and asks for confirmation:
"You are about to create a supervised clock-in for [Officer Name] at [Site Name].
Please confirm the officer is physically present at the site. Type CONFIRM to proceed."
Manager types CONFIRM.

CLOCKBOT FORCED CLOCK-IN:
ClockBot creates a time entry for the officer with:
clockInTime: current timestamp.
clockInMethod: supervisor_override.
supervisorId: the manager who initiated the override.
gps_verification_status: supervisor_confirmed.
A note in the time entry: "Clock-in created by supervisor override via ClockBot.
Initiated by [Manager Name] at [Timestamp]."

ClockBot notifies the officer via in-platform notification:
"Your manager has clocked you in for your shift at [Site Name].
Clock-in time recorded as [Time]. Contact your supervisor with any questions."

ClockBot posts in the shift room:
"ClockBot — Supervised clock-in recorded for [Officer Name] at [Time].
Method: supervisor override. Authorized by [Manager Name].
This event is logged in the audit trail."

CLOCKBOT AUDIT TRAIL:
Every ClockBot action is logged in the universal audit trail with:
Action type: supervisor_clock_in_override.
Officer ID, manager ID, site ID, workspace ID.
Timestamp and clock-in method.
This creates full accountability for every forced clock-in.

CLOCKBOT CANNOT:
Clock out an officer — only the officer can clock themselves out.
Create a clock-in for a shift that has already ended.
Create a clock-in for an officer who is already clocked in.
Be used by an employee or org_admin — only manager, co_owner, or org_owner.

The bots do not operate independently — they operate as instruments of Trinity.

Trinity directs HelpAI to proactively offer resources if an officer reports a dangerous situation.

"[X] shifts currently active. [Y] officers on duty. [Z] incidents reported in last 24 hours."
