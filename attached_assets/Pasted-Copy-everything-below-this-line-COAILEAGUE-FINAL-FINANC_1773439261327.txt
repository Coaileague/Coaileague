The invoice is created in draft status before being sent — not sent automatically without review.
The invoice record contains: client_id, workspace_id, billing_period_start,

delivery_confirmed updates to true when the Resend delivery webhook fires.
If delivery fails the invoice remains in sent status with delivery_confirmed false.
The delivery failure is logged and the org_owner is alerted.

OVERPAYMENTS:

SECTION 3 — PAYROLL CALCULATION FULL AUDIT

Read every payroll-related file: payrollRoutes.ts, payrollService.ts,
payrollCalculationService.ts, and any related files.

PAYROLL PERIOD DETECTION:
Verify the fix applied this session — detectPayPeriod now reads
billingSettingsBlob.payrollCycle correctly.
Create test payroll runs for each cycle type and confirm:
Weekly cycle: pay period spans exactly 7 days.
Bi-weekly cycle: pay period spans exactly 14 days.
Semi-monthly cycle: pay period spans first to fifteenth or sixteenth to end of month.
Monthly cycle: pay period spans first to last day of month.
For each cycle confirm the start date and end date are calculated correctly
and no hours from outside the pay period are included.

HOURS CALCULATION:
For each employee in the payroll run confirm:
Only approved time entries are included — pending or draft entries are excluded.
Time entries are filtered by the exact pay period date range.
Decimal hours are handled correctly — 8 hours 30 minutes equals 8.5 hours.
Overnight shifts that cross midnight are counted correctly in the correct pay period.
A shift from 23:00 on the last day of the period to 07:00 the next period —
confirm the hours before midnight count in the current period and
hours after midnight count in the next period, or document which behavior is implemented.

GROSS PAY CALCULATION:
For hourly employees: gross pay equals total hours multiplied by hourly rate.
Confirm the hourly rate used is from the employee record at time of payroll run —
not a rate that was changed after the pay period ended.
If an employee had a rate change mid-period document how it is handled.
For salaried employees: gross pay equals annual salary divided by pay periods per year.
Confirm the divisor is correct for each pay cycle:
Weekly: 52 pay periods.
Bi-weekly: 26 pay periods.
Semi-monthly: 24 pay periods.
Monthly: 12 pay periods.

OVERTIME CALCULATION:
Does the system calculate overtime for hourly employees?
If yes: overtime threshold is 40 hours per week for federal FLSA compliance.
Confirm hours above 40 in a single workweek are calculated at 1.5x the regular rate.
Confirm the workweek definition — Sunday to Saturday or Monday to Sunday — document which.
If overtime is not calculated confirm this is a known limitation and flag it.

1099 CONTRACTOR HANDLING:
For contractors confirm:
No federal or state tax withholding is calculated — contractors handle their own taxes.
The payment amount equals agreed rate multiplied by hours with no deductions.
The payment is tracked separately from W-2 employee payroll.
Contractors who reach $600 in calendar year payments are flagged for 1099-NEC reporting.
Confirm the $600 threshold tracking exists in the code.

TAX WITHHOLDING FOR W-2 EMPLOYEES:
Does the platform calculate federal income tax withholding based on W-4 data?
Does it calculate Social Security at 6.2 percent up to the annual wage base?
Does it calculate Medicare at 1.45 percent with no wage base limit?
Does it calculate state income tax withholding for Texas?
Note: Texas has no state income tax — confirm the platform does not withhold
state income tax for Texas-based employees.
If tax calculations exist confirm they use current IRS tables not hardcoded rates.
If tax calculations are not implemented confirm this is documented and the
org_owner is informed they are responsible for tax calculations outside the platform.

PAYROLL APPROVAL GATE:
Confirm the payroll run cannot execute without explicit org_owner approval.
Confirm there is no code path that auto-approves a payroll run.
Confirm session expiry during payroll review leaves the run in pending state.
Confirm two concurrent approval attempts are blocked by a race condition guard.

PAYROLL RECORD LOCKING:
After a payroll run is approved confirm:
The payroll run record is locked — status cannot be changed back to pending.
Individual paycheck records cannot be edited after approval.
An attempt to edit an approved payroll record via direct API call is rejected.
The rejection must happen at the route handler level not just the UI.

SECTION 6 — EDGE CASES THAT BREAK BILLING

Test every dangerous edge case that could cause revenue loss or overpayment.

EDGE CASE 1 — INVOICE CREATED WITH ZERO HOURS:
Attempt to generate an invoice when a client has zero approved time entries.
Confirm the system either blocks the invoice creation or creates a $0 invoice clearly marked.
A $0 invoice sent to a client is unprofessional. Confirm it cannot happen silently.

EDGE CASE 2 — DUPLICATE INVOICE GENERATION:
Attempt to generate an invoice for the same client covering the same billing period twice.
Confirm the second generation is blocked with a message that an invoice
already exists for this period.
Confirm no duplicate invoices can be created for the same billing period.

EDGE CASE 3 — TIME ENTRY USED IN MULTIPLE INVOICES:
Confirm a time entry that has already been invoiced cannot be included in a second invoice.
The time entry should be marked as invoiced after the first invoice is generated.
If a time entry can be double-invoiced that is a direct revenue integrity failure.

EDGE CASE 4 — RATE CHANGE AFTER INVOICE GENERATED:
If an org_owner changes a client's billing rate after an invoice has been sent,
does the sent invoice update to the new rate?
It must not. A sent invoice must lock its rate at generation time.
Confirm the invoice stores the rate at which it was generated not a reference to the current rate.

EDGE CASE 5 — EMPLOYEE PAY RATE CHANGE MID-PERIOD:
If an employee's hourly rate changes mid-pay-period which rate is used for payroll?
Document the exact behavior from the code.
If it uses the rate at end of period employees could receive more or less than they earned.
The correct behavior is to calculate hours before the change at the old rate
and hours after the change at the new rate, or to use the rate at start of period.
Report what the code actually does and flag it if it could cause underpayment.

EDGE CASE 6 — TIMEZONE HANDLING:
If an officer clocks in at 23:45 in Central Time and the server is in UTC,
is the clock-in time stored in UTC and displayed in the org's local timezone?
Confirm all timestamps are stored in UTC and converted for display.
A timezone bug could put hours in the wrong pay period.

EDGE CASE 7 — DELETED CLIENT WITH OPEN INVOICES:
If a client is deleted or marked inactive with outstanding unpaid invoices,
are those invoices still accessible and collectable?
Confirm open invoices survive client deactivation.
Confirm collections escalation continues on overdue invoices for inactive clients
or that the org_owner is explicitly notified to handle them manually.

EDGE CASE 8 — REFUND HANDLING:
If a Stripe refund is issued for an already-paid invoice what happens?
Does the invoice status revert?
Does the revenue ledger entry get reversed?
Does the org_owner receive a notification?
Confirm a Stripe refund does not silently disappear from the financial records.

EDGE CASE 9 — CREDITS PACK PURCHASE FAILURE:
If a credits pack purchase fails mid-transaction —
Stripe charges the card but the credits allocation webhook fails —
are the credits still allocated when the webhook retries?
Confirm the idempotency key on the credits pack webhook prevents double-allocation
on retry while also ensuring allocation eventually completes on failure.

EDGE CASE 10 — STATEWIDE WORKSPACE RECEIVES A STRIPE WEBHOOK:
If a Stripe webhook event somehow references the Statewide workspace ID
confirm the billing_exempt check fires before any credit allocation or charge processing.
Confirm no Stripe charges are ever created for the Statewide workspace under any scenario.
Run this test now by checking whether a Stripe customer record even exists for Statewide.
If no customer record exists that is the strongest possible protection — confirm it.
