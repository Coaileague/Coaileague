**COAILEAGUE SUPPORT ORGANIZATION SEMANTIC AUDIT**

This audit verifies that CoAIleague's internal support infrastructure — Trinity, HelpAI, system bots, root admin, and human support agents — operates as a fully functional autonomous support organization. The goal is 99% automated resolution by Trinity and her orchestration, 1% human escalation. Every tool, every action, every CRUD command must be wired, persist to the database, execute live, and operate under strict RBAC.

Ask every question. Find the code. Verify it works. Fix what does not. Report everything.

**All tests against ACME sandbox. Statewide untouchable.**

**THE CORE SEMANTIC QUESTIONS FOR SUPPORT ORGANIZATION:**

```
Q1: Can Trinity identify who is calling for help
    and what workspace they belong to
    before taking any action?

Q2: Can Trinity read the full context of any org —
    their officers, schedules, invoices, compliance,
    documents, settings — to understand their situation
    without asking them to repeat themselves?

Q3: Can Trinity actually fix problems — not just describe them —
    by writing to the database, updating records,
    resending notifications, correcting settings,
    and confirming the fix worked?

Q4: Can Trinity override end-user settings when needed
    to resolve issues — for example resetting a PIN,
    unlocking an account, fixing a broken notification preference —
    without the user having to do anything?

Q5: Can human support agents do everything Trinity can do
    but with a UI — a support console that shows them
    the full workspace context and lets them
    take corrective actions that persist?

Q6: Are all support actions attributed to the correct actor —
    Trinity system actor for automated fixes,
    support agent user ID for human fixes —
    with a full audit trail?

Q7: Can the support system escalate correctly —
    Trinity attempts resolution first,
    logs what she tried,
    then hands off to human with full context
    if she cannot resolve?

Q8: Does the org owner know when support intervened
    in their workspace — full transparency,
    notification of every support action taken?

Q9: Are support agents and Trinity strictly limited
    to read and corrective actions —
    they cannot delete financial records,
    cannot access data outside their scope,
    cannot impersonate users maliciously?

Q10: Does every support interaction produce a ticket
     with full transcript, actions taken,
     resolution status, and time to resolution?
```
