STEP 2 — TRINITY BRAIN TOOLS FOR EACH BOT

Every bot must use Trinity's actual intelligence — not hardcoded responses,
not template strings, not random selections. Trinity thinks. The bots execute.

REPORTBOT — TRINITY BRAIN WIRING:

ReportBot must use Trinity's AI to do these things with real intelligence:

Photo analysis: when an officer posts a photo ReportBot must pass it to
Trinity's vision capabilities to analyze what is in the photo.
Trinity should note: is this a site perimeter, a gate, a lobby, a parking area?
Are there any visible anomalies — graffiti, damage, suspicious items?
Is the lighting consistent with the reported time of day?
ReportBot includes Trinity's brief analysis in the log entry.
This is not optional decoration — it creates accountability and a richer audit trail.

Incident assessment: when an officer posts any message Trinity analyzes it
for incident indicators using actual AI language understanding, not keyword matching.
Trinity assesses: is this routine, noteworthy, or a reportable incident?
If noteworthy Trinity prompts ReportBot to ask follow-up questions.
If reportable Trinity directs ReportBot to escalate immediately.
The assessment must use the trinityChatService or unifiedGeminiClient
with the shift context — site name, post orders, officer name — as context.

Report compilation: at end of shift ReportBot must use Trinity's intelligence
to rewrite officer messages into professional security report language.
The AI must understand context — "guy was weird" becomes
"An unidentified male subject exhibited suspicious behavior at the main gate."
The officer's original words are preserved in a footnote.
This must go through the AI model — not a template substitution system.

Quality scan: before generating the PDF Trinity reads the full compiled report
and identifies any remaining issues — inconsistencies, missing required fields,
unclear incident descriptions. ReportBot presents these to the officer or manager
for resolution before the PDF is locked.

HELPAI — TRINITY BRAIN WIRING:

HelpAI must use Trinity's full workspace-scoped intelligence to answer questions.
HelpAI is not a FAQ bot. HelpAI has access to real data and real intelligence.

When an officer asks about post orders HelpAI must retrieve the actual post orders
document from the document safe for that specific site and summarize it intelligently.
Not a generic "check your post orders" response. The actual content.

When an officer asks about their schedule HelpAI must query the actual shift records
and return the officer's specific upcoming shifts with site names and times.

When an officer reports a medical emergency HelpAI must:
Immediately provide first aid guidance using Trinity's AI knowledge.
Retrieve the site's emergency contact numbers from the site record.
Alert the manager via an autonomous notification — not wait to be asked.
Suggest calling 911 and staying on the line.
The manager notification must fire within 10 seconds of the emergency mention.

When an officer asks a company policy question HelpAI must:
Search the uploaded employee handbook for that org if one exists.
If the handbook is uploaded and digitized Trinity reads it and answers from it.
If no handbook is uploaded HelpAI says so and suggests the officer contact their manager.

HelpAI must know what it does not know and say so clearly.
If asked about another employee HelpAI refuses with a specific explanation.
If asked about financial or billing data HelpAI refuses and redirects.
If asked something completely outside its domain HelpAI redirects to relevant help.

CLOCKBOT — TRINITY BRAIN WIRING:

ClockBot must use Trinity's intelligence to validate every supervised clock-in.

Before executing a supervisor override ClockBot must check through Trinity:
Is the officer currently assigned to a shift at this site right now?
Is the officer's license active and not expired?
Is the officer's onboarding status complete?
Has the shift already ended — if yes ClockBot refuses to clock in.
Is the officer already clocked in somewhere else — if yes ClockBot flags this.

ClockBot presents all of this context to the manager before the CONFIRM step:
"Officer Marcus Johnson is assigned to Lone Star Industrial Park from 22:00 to 06:00.
His Texas Guard Card is active through June 2027. Onboarding complete.
Type CONFIRM to create a supervised clock-in at [current time]."

After the override ClockBot uses Trinity to generate a brief note for the audit:
"Supervised clock-in created by [Manager Name] at [Time]. Officer [Name] was present
at [Site] but unable to complete GPS clock-in. Reason logged."
ClockBot asks the manager for the reason for the override and records it.
The reason field is required — ClockBot does not proceed without a reason.

MEETINGBOT — TRINITY BRAIN WIRING:

MeetingBot must use Trinity's intelligence to generate useful meeting summaries.

During the meeting MeetingBot must track discussion threads intelligently.
Not just action items tagged with @MeetingBot — it must understand the
full conversation and identify key points that were discussed even if not tagged.

At meeting end Trinity reads the full transcript and generates:
A structured summary of what was discussed in plain professional language.
All decisions made even if not explicitly tagged.
All action items with assigned owners inferred from context.
A list of unresolved questions that need follow-up.
The estimated next steps based on what was discussed.

The summary must be generated by the AI model — not assembled from tagged items only.
The meeting transcript is passed to Trinity with context about the org and the attendees.
Trinity returns a structured JSON with summary, decisions, action items, next steps.
MeetingBot formats this into the PDF.

STEP 3 — DOCUMENT PIPELINE FULL AUDIT AND BUILD

Every document that CoAIleague mentions anywhere must be fully functional
from generation through signing through storage. No stubs. No placeholder UIs.

EMPLOYEE SECURITY PACKET — TEXAS PSB COMPLIANT:

This packet must be generated automatically when a new employee is created.
Trinity orchestrates the generation — it does not wait for the org_owner to click anything.

The packet must contain these documents in this exact order.
Each document must be generated with real employee data — no blank fields.

Document 1 — Employment Application:
Full name, DOB, address, phone, email, SSN field (masked after entry),
emergency contact, position, start date, employment type, prior security experience,
Texas PSB license number if applicable, background check consent signature,
drug screening consent signature, applicant signature and date.
Routing: applicant signs first. HR or org_owner countersigns.

Document 2 — Employment Offer Letter:
Employee name, position title, start date, pay rate and type, schedule description,
reporting manager, at-will clause, contingencies (background check, drug test, license).
Routing: org_owner signs first. Employee countersigns as acceptance.

Document 3 — W-4 Employee Withholding Certificate:
Must match IRS W-4 format exactly. Name, address, SSN masked, filing status,
dependents, other income, deductions, extra withholding, employee signature.
Legal content must not be stylized — only the surrounding design chrome.
Routing: employee signs only. HR receives.

Document 4 — I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification:
Must match USCIS I-9 format exactly. Section 1 by employee, Section 2 by employer.
Section 1: name, address, DOB, SSN, citizenship attestation, employee signature.
Section 2: document type, issuing authority, document number, expiry, employer cert.
Routing: employee completes Section 1. HR completes Section 2.

Document 5 — Direct Deposit Authorization:
Employee name, bank name, account type, routing number, account number,
deposit amount or percentage, employee signature.
Routing: employee signs. Payroll receives.

Document 6 — Texas Security Officer License Verification:
Employee name, Texas PSB license number, class, expiration date,
confirmation that license must stay active, employee acknowledgment,
HR countersignature confirming verification completed.

Document 7 — Employee Handbook Acknowledgment:
If the org has uploaded a handbook: digitize it, require scroll to bottom
before signature becomes available, employee signs and initials.
If no handbook uploaded: placeholder with instruction to upload one.
Routing: employee signs only.

Document 8 — At-Will Employment Agreement:
Standard Texas at-will statement. Employee and org_owner both sign.

Document 9 — Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreement:
Security-industry specific. Client identities, site locations, patrol routes,
access codes, incident reports all defined as confidential.
Both parties sign.

Document 10 — Background Check Authorization:
Standalone full authorization. Name history, address history 7 years, SSN masked.
Employee signs.

PACKET PIPELINE:
Trinity generates all 10 documents within 5 minutes of employee creation.
Each document is pre-populated with data already in the system — no re-entry.
Each document is routed to the correct first signer automatically.
Each signer receives an email with a Sign Now button.
The signing sequence is enforced — no document can skip the queue.
After all 10 are executed: employee onboarding_status updates to completed.
Trinity becomes aware the employee is scheduling-eligible.
Both the org document safe and the employee personal portal receive copies.
Every document is WORM locked after execution.

CONTRACTOR PACKET — 4 DOCUMENTS:

Document 1 — Independent Contractor Agreement:
Contractor name or business, EIN or SSN, scope, rate, payment schedule,
independent status affirmation, no benefits clause, Texas law.
Both parties sign.

Document 2 — W-9 Request for Taxpayer Identification:
Must match IRS W-9 format exactly. Contractor signs only. Org retains.

Document 3 — Texas Security Contractor License Verification:
Same structure as employee license verification but contractor-specific language.

Document 4 — Contractor Confidentiality Agreement:
Same content as employee NDA but explicitly states contractor not employee status.
Both parties sign.

EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK DIGITIZATION PIPELINE:

Org_owner uploads a PDF handbook.
Trinity processes it — converts to scrollable online document.
The scroll position is tracked — the signature button only appears
after the employee has scrolled to 100 percent of the document.
The scroll enforcement is server-verified — not just CSS. The employee's
scroll completion is recorded in the database before the signature is accepted.
Trinity tracks which version of the handbook each employee signed.
When org_owner uploads a new version Trinity automatically identifies all
employees who signed the previous version and sends them the new version.
Both old and new versions retained in the document safe permanently.

POST ORDERS PIPELINE:

Post orders are created per client site by the org_owner or manager.
Post orders contain: site name, address, coverage hours, specific duties,
access procedures, emergency contacts, equipment issued, special instructions.
When post orders are created Trinity automatically sends them to all officers
assigned to that site for acknowledgment signature.
Officers must sign post orders before Trinity allows them to be scheduled at that site.
When post orders are updated Trinity identifies all assigned officers and
sends the new version for re-acknowledgment before their next shift.
Both old and new versions retained permanently.
Post orders acknowledgment status is visible on the officer's compliance profile.

SALES PROPOSAL AND CONTRACT PIPELINE:

The pipeline has 7 stages. Trinity manages every stage transition.

Stage 1 — Lead Capture:
org_owner adds a lead with client name, contact, service type, estimated hours.
Trinity adds it to the pipeline dashboard immediately.
Trinity drafts an initial proposal within 60 seconds of lead creation.
The draft is surfaced to the org_owner for review — not auto-sent.

Stage 2 — Proposal Generation and Send:
Trinity selects the closest proposal template for the service type.
Trinity populates every field from existing org data:
License number, insurance, officer qualifications, proposed schedule, billing rate.
The proposal total is calculated automatically — hours times rate.
org_owner reviews in the document system, edits if needed, approves.
On approval Trinity sends the proposal via Track B branded email.
Email subject: Security Services Proposal from [Security Company Name].
Content: professional cover letter, proposal summary, View Full Proposal button.
Trinity records send timestamp. Lead moves to proposal_sent stage.

Stage 3 — Automated Follow-Up:
Day 5 no response: Trinity drafts follow-up for org_owner approval.
Day 10 no response: Trinity drafts second follow-up — different angle.
Day 15 no response: Trinity alerts org_owner to make a phone call. Drafts a call script.
Day 20 no response: Trinity moves lead to stalled. Org_owner decides continue or close.
ALL follow-up emails require org_owner approval before Trinity sends.
Trinity drafts. Human approves. Trinity sends. No exceptions.

Stage 4 — Negotiation:
Client requests changes. Manager logs client response.
Trinity generates revised proposal with incremented version number.
Every version stored permanently — none deleted.

Stage 5 — Contract Generation:
Client accepts. Manager marks accepted.
Trinity auto-generates the full service agreement from accepted proposal terms.
No re-entry of terms. Everything carries over.
org_owner reviews in the document signing portal.
org_owner signs first. Trinity sends contract to client.
Client receives Track B branded email with Sign Contract button.

Stage 6 — Contract Execution and Client Activation:
Client signs. Contract executes. Document safe receives master copy.
Trinity automatically creates:
Client record in clients table.
Site records for all sites in the contract.
Billing rate configured from contract terms.
Task list for org_owner: configure post orders and geofences.
Pipeline card moves to active_client.
Client appears in active clients list for scheduling and billing.
Trinity posts new client activated to the Briefing Channel.

Stage 7 — Renewal:
90 days before expiration Trinity alerts org_owner in morning briefing.
Trinity offers to generate renewal proposal from current contract terms.
On approval Trinity begins Stage 2 through 6 again automatically.
