Cost Attribution — Orgs pay for their own AI usage:Every AI call Trinity makes should be tagged with the workspace ID. Your platform already has a credits/metering system (you mentioned AI cost tracking early on). The rule is simple: if Trinity is doing work FOR an org (scanning their clients, scheduling their employees, analyzing their data), that org's token meter gets charged. The platform only absorbs costs for platform-level operations like system health checks or platform-wide analytics.Scan Efficiency — Don't scan what hasn't changed:The every-30-minutes full scan is wasteful. Here's what I'd recommend instead:Event-driven scanning instead of polling. Trinity shouldn't scan on a timer. She should react to changes. Think of it like this — she's currently a security guard walking the entire building every 30 minutes. She should be watching the security cameras and only going to the floor where the alarm went off.Specifically: when a client's settings change (staffing demands, schedule, rates), the settings update endpoint fires a client_settings_changed event. Trinity hears it and only re-evaluates THAT client. When an employee's availability changes, same pattern — only re-evaluate shifts that employee is involved in. When a shift is manually created or a call-off happens, Trinity evaluates just that gap.Keep the cron but make it lightweight. The 30-minute cron stays, but instead of a full AI-powered analysis of every client, it does a cheap database-only check: "has anything changed since my last scan?" Compare a last_modified timestamp on clients, employees, and shifts against Trinity's last_scan_at timestamp. If nothing changed, exit immediately — zero tokens burned. If 2 clients changed, only process those 2.Three tiers of intelligence:Tier 1 is free and uses no AI tokens. Pure database queries — checking for changes, counting unfilled shifts, detecting OT thresholds. Just SQL. This runs every 30 minutes and costs nothing.Tier 2 is lightweight and uses minimal tokens. Simple AI decisions like "which employee best fits this shift" using scoring math. This only fires when Tier 1 detects something that needs action.Tier 3 is full reasoning and uses real tokens. Trinity's deep analysis — pattern recognition, compliance review, natural language explanations for the thought stream. This only fires for complex scenarios like full schedule generation, conflict resolution, or when a manager asks Trinity to "fix the schedule."Most of the time Trinity operates at Tier 1.
