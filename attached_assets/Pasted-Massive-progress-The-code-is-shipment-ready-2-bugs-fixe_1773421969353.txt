PART 2 — TRINITY STAFFING EMAIL SERVICE

Trinity offers a staffing communication service for security company operators.
This is not just notification emails. This is a full staffing outreach system
that Trinity manages on behalf of the org_owner.

WHAT THIS SERVICE DOES:

Available Officer Broadcast:
When a shift needs coverage and no internal officer is available,
org_owner or manager can instruct Trinity to broadcast the opportunity
to all available officers who meet the post requirements.
Trinity drafts the broadcast message, the manager reviews and approves it,
Trinity sends it via email and in-platform notification simultaneously.
Officers reply by tapping an Accept Shift button in the email or platform.
Trinity reports back to the manager with who accepted and who did not respond
within the response window.

Shift Confirmation Reminders:
48 hours before every shift Trinity sends a reminder to the assigned officer
via email and in-platform notification.
The reminder includes: shift date, start time, site name, site address,
post orders summary, uniform requirements, supervisor contact.
If the officer does not acknowledge the reminder within 24 hours Trinity
escalates to the manager.

Call-Off Response Sequence:
When an officer submits a call-off Trinity automatically sends the following:
To the calling officer: confirmation that call-off was received, who their
supervisor is, and that they will be in touch if there are any issues.
To the manager: immediate notification with the open shift details and
Trinity's replacement candidate list ready to review.
To the replacement officer when assigned: the shift details, site address,
post orders, and a confirm acceptance link.

New Post Announcement:
When a new client site is added and post orders are created Trinity can
send a new post announcement to all officers who are qualified and available
for that post type, inviting them to indicate availability.

Weekly Schedule Distribution:
When a schedule is published Trinity automatically emails every officer
their individual schedule for the upcoming week.
Each officer receives only their own schedule — not the full org schedule.
The email includes each shift with date, time, site, post title, and any
special instructions for that shift.
A View Full Schedule button links to their personal schedule portal.

Mass Communication Tool:
org_owner can compose a message and send it to: all employees,
all employees at a specific site, all managers, or a custom selection.
Trinity drafts a professional version of whatever the org_owner writes,
the org_owner reviews and approves, then Trinity sends it.
Every mass communication is logged in the audit trail with who sent it,
who received it, and the content.

PART 3 — PROPOSAL AND CONTRACT SALES PIPELINE

This is Trinity's sales intelligence layer for security company operators.
Security companies win business through proposals and contracts.
Trinity manages the entire pipeline from lead to executed contract.

PIPELINE STAGES:

Stage 1 — LEAD:
A potential client is added to the system.
Fields: client name, contact name, contact email, contact phone,
site address or addresses, type of security service needed,
estimated coverage hours, decision timeline, how they were referred.
Trinity flags this as a new lead and adds it to the pipeline dashboard.

Stage 2 — PROPOSAL SENT:
org_owner or Trinity generates a professional service proposal.
The proposal is generated from a template populated with real org data:
org name, license number, insurance information, officer qualifications,
proposed coverage schedule, billing rate, and total monthly value.
The proposal is emailed to the client contact with a professional cover letter.
Trinity tracks when the proposal was sent and when it was opened.
If the proposal has not been responded to in 5 business days Trinity
sends a follow-up email and alerts the org_owner.

Stage 3 — PROPOSAL FOLLOW-UP:
Trinity manages follow-up automatically.
Day 5: polite follow-up email to client — just checking in on the proposal.
Day 10: second follow-up with a different angle — any questions or concerns.
Day 15: Trinity alerts the org_owner directly and suggests a phone call.
Day 20: Trinity moves the lead to stalled status and alerts the org_owner
to decide whether to continue pursuing or close the lead.
All follow-up emails are reviewed and approved by org_owner before sending.
Trinity drafts them. Humans approve them. Trinity sends them.

Stage 4 — NEGOTIATION:
Client responds with questions or requests changes.
Trinity logs the communication thread.
org_owner can instruct Trinity to revise the proposal terms.
Trinity generates a revised proposal version and tracks the version history.
Every revision is stored — no version is ever deleted.

Stage 5 — CONTRACT SENT:
Client accepts the proposal.
Trinity automatically generates the full service agreement from the accepted proposal terms.
org_owner reviews and signs first in the platform.
Trinity sends the contract to the client for their signature via the document pipeline.

Stage 6 — CONTRACT EXECUTED:
Client signs. Contract is executed and stored in the document safe.
Trinity moves the client from lead to active client in the system.
Trinity automatically creates the client record, the site records,
and prompts the org_owner to configure billing rates and post orders.
Trinity schedules the first operational briefing for the new site.

Stage 7 — RENEWAL:
90 days before contract expiration Trinity alerts the org_owner.
Trinity offers to generate a renewal proposal automatically.
If org_owner approves Trinity generates the renewal proposal and begins
the Stage 2 through 6 flow again.

PIPELINE DASHBOARD:
A visual pipeline view showing all leads and active contracts by stage.
Each card shows: client name, stage, days in current stage, contract value,
last action taken, next action due, and Trinity's recommended next step.
Filter by stage, by contract value, by days in pipeline, by assigned manager.
Click any card to see the full lead or contract history.

TRINITY INTELLIGENCE IN THE PIPELINE:
Trinity proactively moves items through the pipeline based on time rules.
She surfaces the pipeline in the daily Org Operations Briefing:
How many active proposals are outstanding.
How many leads are stalled.
Total pipeline value.
Contracts up for renewal in the next 90 days.
Trinity can draft any email in the pipeline on command — the human always approves.
Trinity tracks email open rates if Resend tracking is enabled and reports back.

PROPOSAL TEMPLATE LIBRARY:
org_owner can build and save proposal templates for different service types:
Armed patrol, unarmed patrol, event security, executive protection,
access control, alarm response, mobile patrol, and so on.
Each template has pre-filled language for that service type that the
org_owner can customize.
When generating a proposal Trinity selects the closest template and
customizes it with the specific client and site details.
