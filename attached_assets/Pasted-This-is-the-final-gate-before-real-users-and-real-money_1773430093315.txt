SEAM 4 — CLOCK-IN → TIME ENTRY → TIMESHEET → PAYROLL

This is the core financial chain. Every step must hand off correctly
to the next or hours get lost and pay is wrong.

Trace the exact path:
Officer clocks in — time_entry record created with status active,
clockInTime, clockInLatitude, clockInLongitude, employeeId, shiftId, siteId.
Officer clocks out — time_entry record updated with clockOutTime,
totalHours calculated server-side not client-side, status changes to completed.
Pay period closes — timesheets auto-generate from all completed time_entries
for that pay period. Confirm no time entry is missed or double-counted.
Timesheet shows correct total hours for each employee matching the sum
of their individual time_entry records exactly.
Manager approves timesheet — timesheet status locks, cannot be edited.
Payroll calculation runs from approved timesheets — gross pay equals
total approved hours multiplied by pay_rate from the employee record.
Confirm the pay_rate used is from the employee record at time of calculation
not a hardcoded value and not a rate that could have been changed mid-period.
Pay stub generates with correct gross pay, hours, pay period dates, and employee name.
Pay stub notification email fires to the employee with the correct pay period.

Test this entire chain with one Acme employee doing a full cycle right now.
A discrepancy anywhere in this chain means officers get paid wrong amounts.
That is a payroll liability. Find and fix any discrepancy before go-live.
