PART 1 — UNIVERSAL DOCUMENT DESIGN SYSTEM
One design standard. Every document looks like it came from the same professional system.
No document looks different from another. No mobile document looks worse than desktop.

DESIGN STANDARDS — APPLY TO EVERY DOCUMENT:

Header: every document has a uniform header containing the org logo or name,
org license number, document title, document ID, version number, date generated,
and a confidentiality classification label. Header is identical in structure
across all document types. Only the content changes.

Footer: every document has a uniform footer containing page number of total pages,
org name, platform-generated watermark "Powered by CoAIleague" in subtle gray,
document status (Draft, Pending Signature, Executed, Voided), and a QR code
linking to the document in the org document safe for verification purposes.

Typography: one font family across all documents. Clean, professional, readable.
Headings are consistent sizes. Body text is consistent size. Line height is
generous for readability. No document uses more than two font weights.

Signature areas: every signature block is identical in structure.
Full legal name field, signature field, date field, title or role field.
Each signer gets their own clearly labeled signature block.
Signature blocks have a visible border and a distinct background color
to make them unmistakable on both screen and printed output.

Color system: one primary brand color used for headers and section dividers.
Neutral gray for secondary elements. White background always.
No garish colors. Professional security industry aesthetic.

Auto-sizing: document content expands to fill content naturally.
No content is ever cut off. No text overflows its container.
Signature blocks always appear on a clean page — never split across a page break.
If a signature block would be split by a page break force a page break before it.

MOBILE RENDERING:
Every document renders correctly on screens as small as 375px wide.
Signature areas are finger-tap sized on mobile — minimum 44px height.
The signature capture on mobile accepts finger drawing, not just typed name.
Pinch-to-zoom works on all document views.
Documents can be scrolled vertically on mobile without horizontal scroll.
The sign button is always visible and accessible — never buried below the fold.

DESKTOP RENDERING:
Documents render in a centered reading column on wide screens.
Maximum content width of 800px for readability.
A document action sidebar shows on the right for desktop — current signers,
status, pending actions, download button, audit trail link.
Print layout matches digital layout exactly — what you see is what prints.

PDF GENERATION:
Every document can be exported as a professional-grade PDF.
The PDF matches the on-screen rendering exactly.
PDFs are generated server-side not client-side.
PDF file names follow this format: DocumentType_OrgName_Date_DocumentID.pdf
PDFs are automatically saved to the org document safe on generation.
PDFs are watermarked with EXECUTED or COPY across the body when finalized.

PART 2 — UNIVERSAL DOCUSIGN ORCHESTRATION PIPELINE
One pipeline. All documents flow through it. Trinity orchestrates every step.

THE UNIVERSAL DOCUMENT PIPELINE — 8 STEPS:

Step 1 TRIGGER: Document creation is triggered by a platform event, a Trinity action,
a manual org_owner or manager action, or an inbound request.
Every document creation event is logged in the audit trail immediately on trigger.

Step 2 GENERATE: The document template is populated with the relevant data.
Employee name, org name, license number, dates, pay rates, site details,
whatever the document type requires — all pulled from the database automatically.
No manual data entry for fields the system already knows.
The document is rendered and stored as a draft in the document safe.

Step 3 ROUTE: Trinity determines who needs to sign or acknowledge the document
based on the document type and the workflow rules for that type.
The routing is deterministic — the same document type always goes to the same
set of signers in the same order. No ambiguity.

Step 4 NOTIFY: Each signer receives a notification via the notification panel,
an email via Resend with the document title, a summary of what they are signing,
and a direct link to the document. The notification includes a clear deadline
if one is required by the document type.

Step 5 SIGN OR ACKNOWLEDGE: The signer opens the document, reviews it,
and either signs, initials, or acknowledges each required field.
The platform captures their full legal name, signature, timestamp, IP address,
and device type for each signature event. This is the legal record.
Signers can only sign their own designated signature blocks — not other signers blocks.

Step 6 VALIDATE: After each signature the platform validates that the signature
block is complete and that the signer is who they claim to be based on their
authenticated session. An unauthenticated signature is never accepted.

Step 7 COMPLETE: When all required signers have signed the document status changes
to Executed. The document is locked — WORM enforced. No further edits are possible.
A final executed copy is generated as a PDF with all signatures visible.
The executed PDF is saved to the org document safe permanently.

Step 8 DISTRIBUTE: Executed copies are automatically distributed to all parties.
The org document safe receives the master copy.
Each signer receives an executed copy via email for their records.
The employee or contractor receives a copy in their personal document portal.
Trinity logs the completion in the audit trail with timestamps for every step.

PIPELINE RULES:
A document that has not been executed cannot be used operationally.
For example: an employee cannot be scheduled until their onboarding packet is executed.
A contractor cannot be assigned to a client site until their contractor agreement is executed.
These gates are enforced by Trinity — not just recommended, enforced.

If a document reaches its deadline without all signatures Trinity escalates:
24 hours before deadline: reminder notification and email to all pending signers.
At deadline: URGENT alert to manager and org_owner.
After deadline: document moves to overdue status, Trinity flags for manual resolution.

Decline to sign: if a signer declines the document routes back to the org_owner
with the declination reason. The org_owner decides to void, modify, or re-send.

EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING PACKET — sent as a complete packet to every new hire.
All documents in the packet are sent together and must all be completed
before the employee is considered fully onboarded. The packet contains:

Document 1 — Employee Application:
Fields: full legal name, date of birth, address, phone, email, social security
number (masked after entry), emergency contact name and phone, position applying for,
available start date, employment type (full-time, part-time, on-call),
previous security experience yes or no with description, Texas Security Officer
license number if already licensed, license expiration date if applicable,
background check consent signature, drug screening consent signature,
applicant signature and date.
Routing: applicant signs first, then HR manager or org_owner countersigns.

Document 2 — Employment Offer Letter:
Fields: employee name, position title, start date, pay rate, pay type hourly or salary,
work schedule description, reporting manager name, employment at-will clause,
contingent offer conditions (background check, drug test, license verification).
Routing: org_owner signs first as the offering party, employee signs as acceptance.

Document 3 — W-4 Employee Withholding Certificate:
Standard IRS W-4 format. Fields: name, address, SSN masked, filing status,
multiple jobs checkbox, dependents section, other income and deductions,
extra withholding, employee signature and date.
Note: this is the standard federal form — format must match IRS layout exactly
so it is legally valid. Do not stylize the content, only the surrounding design chrome.
Routing: employee signs only. HR receives completed form.

Document 4 — I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification:
Standard USCIS I-9 format. Section 1 completed by employee: name, address, DOB,
SSN, attestation of citizenship status, employee signature.
Section 2 completed by employer: document title, issuing authority, document number,
expiration date, employer certification signature and date.
Note: same as W-4 — legal form content must match USCIS format exactly.
Routing: employee completes Section 1, HR completes Section 2.

Document 5 — Direct Deposit Authorization:
Fields: employee name, employee ID, bank name, account type checking or savings,
routing number, account number, deposit percentage or full amount,
voided check upload or manual entry confirmation, employee signature and date.
Routing: employee signs, payroll processor or org_owner receives.

Document 6 — Texas Security Officer License Verification:
Fields: employee name, Texas PSB license number, license class, expiration date,
confirmation that employee will maintain active license as condition of employment,
platform auto-pulls license status from PSB if integration is available or
provides a field for manual verification with HR signature confirming verification.
Routing: employee acknowledges, HR countersigns confirming verification completed.

Document 7 — Company Employee Handbook Acknowledgment:
The company handbook can be uploaded as a PDF by the org_owner.
The platform converts it to a readable online document.
The employee must scroll through the entire document before the
acknowledgment signature becomes available — cannot skip to the end.
Fields: employee name, date, signature acknowledging they have read,
understood, and agree to abide by the handbook.
Routing: employee signs acknowledgment only. No org countersignature required.

Document 8 — At-Will Employment Agreement:
Standard Texas at-will employment statement confirming either party may
terminate the employment relationship at any time with or without cause
and with or without notice, subject to applicable law.
Fields: employee name, position, start date, employee signature, org_owner signature.
Routing: both parties sign.

Document 9 — Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreement:
Fields: employee name, position, effective date, definition of confidential information
specific to the security industry (client identities, site locations, security protocols,
patrol routes, access codes, incident reports), obligations of confidentiality,
term of the agreement, consequences of breach, governing law Texas,
employee signature, org_owner signature.
Routing: both parties sign.

Document 10 — Background Check Authorization:
Separate from the application consent. Full standalone authorization for
background check provider. Fields: full legal name, all names used, date of birth,
SSN masked, address history last 7 years, authorization statement, employee signature.
Routing: employee signs. Org sends to background check provider.

CONTRACTOR ONBOARDING PACKET — sent to all 1099 independent contractors.
Different from employee packet because contractors are not employees.

Document 1 — Independent Contractor Agreement:
Fields: contractor legal name or business name, EIN or SSN, address, scope of services,
compensation rate, payment schedule, independent contractor status affirmation
(not an employee, responsible for own taxes, no benefits), Texas law governing,
both party signatures.
Routing: org_owner signs first, contractor countersigns.

Document 2 — W-9 Request for Taxpayer Identification:
Standard IRS W-9 format. Fields: name, business name if different, federal tax
classification, exemptions, address, taxpayer identification number, certification
signature and date.
Note: must match IRS W-9 layout exactly for legal validity.
Routing: contractor signs only. Org retains for tax filing purposes.

Document 3 — Texas Security Contractor License Verification:
Same structure as employee license verification but labeled for contractor.
Routing: contractor acknowledges, org_owner countersigns.

Document 4 — Contractor Confidentiality Agreement:
Same content as employee NDA but with contractor-specific language
clarifying they are an independent contractor not an employee.
Routing: both parties sign.

OPERATIONAL DOCUMENTS — generated during day-to-day operations.

Post Orders Document:
Every client site has a set of post orders — specific instructions for security
officers assigned to that site.
Fields: client name, site name and address, effective date, post title,
hours of coverage, specific duties and responsibilities per post,
access procedures, emergency contact numbers, incident reporting procedures,
equipment issued for this post, officer acknowledgment signature confirming
they have read and understood the post orders.
Routing: org_owner or manager creates and signs. Every officer assigned to
that site must sign acknowledgment before their first shift.
Post orders updates: when post orders are updated every currently assigned
officer must re-sign the new version before their next shift.

Disciplinary Action Form:
Fields: employee name, employee ID, date of incident, description of incident
or performance issue, prior warnings related to this issue yes or no,
disciplinary action being taken (verbal warning, written warning, suspension,
termination), improvement plan if applicable, employee response section,
employee signature and date acknowledging receipt (not agreement),
manager signature, org_owner countersignature for suspension or termination.
Routing: manager drafts, org_owner reviews and approves, employee is notified
and must acknowledge receipt within 24 hours.
Employee receipt acknowledgment is not agreement — the signature line must
clearly state: signing this form acknowledges receipt of this document
and does not constitute agreement with its contents.

Promotion or Pay Rate Change Notice:
Fields: employee name, current position, new position if promotion,
current pay rate, new pay rate, effective date, reason for change,
performance notes, employee signature accepting the change, org_owner signature.
Routing: org_owner signs first as the offering party, employee countersigns.

On-the-Job Training Record:
Fields: employee name, trainer name, training topics covered with date each was completed,
trainee initials per topic confirming completion, trainer signature per topic,
final overall completion signature by both trainee and trainer.
Routing: trainer and trainee both initial each completed topic. Both sign final completion.

License Expiration Notice:
Automatically generated by Trinity when a Texas PSB license is within 60 days
of expiration.
Fields: employee name, license number, expiration date, instructions for renewal,
consequence of operating with expired license (cannot be scheduled for any post),
employee acknowledgment signature that they have been notified.
Routing: Trinity auto-generates. Employee must acknowledge within 48 hours.
At expiration: employee is automatically removed from scheduling eligibility
until license renewal is confirmed.

CLIENT-FACING DOCUMENTS — professional grade, sent to clients.

Service Proposal:
Fields: org name and license number, client name and contact, proposal date,
proposed service description, site or sites to be covered, coverage schedule,
officer qualifications for this contract, equipment provided, response time
guarantees, billing rate and payment terms, validity period of the proposal,
org_owner signature, space for client signature to accept.
Design must be the most polished document in the system. This is a sales document.

Service Agreement / Contract:
Full legal service contract for Texas. Fields: parties (org and client with addresses),
effective date and term, scope of services matching the proposal, billing and
payment terms, late payment penalties, liability limitations, indemnification,
insurance requirements, termination clauses, dispute resolution, governing law Texas,
both party signatures with dates.
Routing: org_owner signs first, client countersigns.

Certificate of Insurance Reference Letter:
Confirmation letter to clients that the security company carries the required
insurance coverage for the services provided.
Fields: org name, license number, client name, site name, coverage types and limits,
insurance provider name, policy number, policy expiration date, org_owner signature.

PART 4 — DOCUMENT SAFE AND PERSONAL PORTAL

ORG DOCUMENT SAFE:
Every executed document is stored in the org document safe permanently.
Organized by category: Employee Documents, Contractor Documents, Client Documents,
Operational Documents, Compliance Documents.
Each document shows: document title, document type, parties involved, date executed,
current status, a download button for the PDF.
Search and filter by employee name, client name, document type, date range, status.
Documents in the safe cannot be edited or deleted — WORM enforced.
Expired or voided documents are retained but clearly labeled.
org_owner and manager can view the safe.
org_admin can view employee documents only.
Employees cannot view the safe — they have their own personal portal.

EMPLOYEE PERSONAL DOCUMENT PORTAL:
Every employee and contractor has a personal document portal in their profile.
It shows only their own documents — nothing from other employees.
Each document shows: document title, date sent, date signed, status, download button.
Employees can download executed copies of all their own documents at any time.
Employees cannot delete any document from their portal.
Employees can see which documents are pending their signature with a prominent
sign now button.

PART 6 — TRINITY DOCUMENT ORCHESTRATION

Trinity must be aware of and actively managing the document system at all times.

Trinity actions to register if not already registered:
document.generate — generate any document type for any employee or client
document.send_for_signature — route a document to the next required signer
document.check_status — report on all pending documents for an org
document.escalate_overdue — escalate documents past their signature deadline
document.compliance_scan — identify any employee or contractor missing required documents
document.license_expiry_scan — identify any officer whose license expires within 60 days
document.post_orders_acknowledgment_scan — identify officers assigned to sites who
have not yet signed the current post orders version

Trinity proactive behaviors:
Daily compliance scan: every day Trinity checks every employee and contractor
for missing or expired documents. Any gap is reported in the Org Operations
Briefing Channel with a deep link to the affected employee record.
License expiry: 60 days before expiration Trinity generates and sends the
License Expiration Notice to the officer automatically.
New hire trigger: when a new employee record is created Trinity automatically
generates and sends the complete onboarding packet within 5 minutes.
New contractor trigger: same as new hire but the contractor packet.
Post orders change trigger: when post orders are updated Trinity automatically
identifies all assigned officers and sends new post orders for re-acknowledgment.
Contract renewal trigger: 90 days before a client service agreement expires
Trinity alerts the org_owner and offers to generate a renewal proposal.

PART 7 — HANDBOOK UPLOAD AND DIGITIZATION

The org_owner can upload a company employee handbook as a PDF.
The platform converts it to a readable online document that employees can scroll through.
The employee cannot reach the acknowledgment signature until they have scrolled
through 100 percent of the document — this is enforced by tracking scroll position,
not on a timer.
The online version is searchable by keyword.
When the handbook is updated the org_owner uploads the new version.
Trinity automatically identifies all employees who signed the previous version
and sends them the new version for re-acknowledgment.
Both the old and new versions are retained in the document safe.
The audit trail shows which version each employee signed and when.
