How invoices/payroll get created (with partners)

Invoices (QuickBooks Online example)

Your platform calls the QuickBooks API to create the invoice in QBO itself.

Result: The invoice exists natively in QBO (just like if they made it manually). Clients can be emailed from QBO, payments reconcile there, and your hub stays in sync via webhooks.

Recommendation for Phase 1: Let QBO be the source of truth for invoices. Your hub shows status, totals, links, and flags anomalies.


Payroll (Gusto example)

Your platform uses the Gusto API to create a payroll run (draft or ready) based on approved hours.

You choose the behavior:

Draft mode (best for 1% oversight): Your hub (or the employer in Gusto) hits “Approve.”

Auto-approve (fully hands-off): Your hub approves via API once your 1% checks are clear.


Result: The payroll exists in Gusto and is visible exactly like a manual run; taxes/filings stay with Gusto.



So yes—they get the autonomous feature: your hub creates and advances the invoice/payroll inside the partner systems via API, while users still see everything in QBO/Gusto like normal.

Do users need to “log in” to partners through your site?

Not with passwords—they “Connect” via OAuth once:

Your site shows “Connect QuickBooks” / “Connect Gusto”.

They authenticate on the partner’s page (OAuth consent).

You store tokens (securely) and thereafter it’s all API + webhooks.

If tokens die, you show “Reconnect”.


Yes: you need those Connect buttons and OAuth flows on your site to enable autonomy for partner users.

What your hub needs (minimum)

1. Settings → Connections

Buttons: Connect QuickBooks, Connect Gusto.

Show connection status, scopes, last sync, “Reconnect.”



2. Data mapping

Choose the QBO customer per client.

Choose items/rates to use on invoices.

Map employees/contractors to Gusto profiles.



3. Automation preferences

Invoices: create per shift / daily / weekly.

Payroll: draft vs auto-approve; pay period rules; overtime rules.

1% oversight: which anomalies force review.



4. Oversight queue (1% review)

Today’s new invoices/payroll runs.

Flags (rate mismatch, missing client, extreme hours, rounding).

Approve all / approve individually → your hub calls partner APIs to finalize.



5. Webhooks + audit trail

QBO: invoice created/paid/voided.

Gusto: payroll created/submitted/paid.

Log who/what/when for every action.





---

Two operating modes (you can support both)

Partner-Native (recommended now):
Create and store records in the partner (QBO/Gusto). Your hub is the brain + UI + audit, partners are the system of record.

Hub-Native (later):
Create invoices/payroll in your own ledger, then sync to partners (or replace partners entirely). More control, more responsibility.


Start Partner-Native for speed/compliance; evolve to Hub-Native when you’re ready to own the finance layer.
