**Problem B — Total Revenue definition is wrong:**
Total Revenue currently shows gross invoice payments received. This is not useful for business decisions. Redefine the metric correctly:

True financial picture requires three layers:

**Layer 1 — Gross Revenue:** Total invoices paid in period

**Layer 2 — Operating Expenses (tracked in platform):**
- Payroll costs (total officer wages paid from payroll runs)
- Platform subscription fee (CoAIleague monthly cost)
- Any vendor payments entered manually

**Layer 3 — External Expenses (from Plaid bank connection or manual entry):**
- Office rent
- Insurance premiums
- Utilities
- Equipment purchases
- Any other categorized business expenses

**Net Operating Income = Gross Revenue − Payroll − Platform Costs − External Expenses**

Build this correctly:

Create `business_expenses` table:
- `id`, `workspace_id`, `expense_category` (enum: payroll, rent, insurance, utilities, equipment, software, marketing, legal, accounting, miscellaneous), `vendor_name`, `amount`, `expense_date`, `description`, `is_recurring`, `recurring_interval` (monthly/quarterly/annually), `plaid_transaction_id` (if from Plaid), `receipt_url`, `created_by`, `created_at`

Create `financial_period_summaries` table:
- `id`, `workspace_id`, `period_start`, `period_end`, `gross_revenue`, `total_payroll`, `total_expenses`, `net_operating_income`, `expense_breakdown` (jsonb by category), `generated_at`

Update the dashboard Total Revenue card to show Net Operating Income with a tooltip explaining the calculation. Show month-over-month comparison. Show year-to-date.

**Problem C — Plaid integration for bank data:**
Build `plaid_connections` table: `id`, `workspace_id`, `access_token` (encrypted), `item_id`, `institution_name`, `account_ids` (text array), `last_sync_at`, `is_active`

Build `plaid_transactions` table: `id`, `workspace_id`, `plaid_connection_id`, `transaction_id`, `account_id`, `amount`, `date`, `merchant_name`, `category` (text array), `is_expense`, `matched_expense_id`, `created_at`

Build Plaid Link flow:
- org_owner can connect their business bank account via Plaid Link
- Trinity pulls transactions nightly and categorizes them as business expenses
- Trinity flags uncategorized transactions for org_owner review
- Plaid transactions automatically populate `business_expenses` with the vendor, amount, and date
- Org_owner can confirm or reclassify each transaction

**If Plaid is not yet integrated** — build a manual expense entry system that captures the same data and add a "Connect Bank Account" button that shows "Plaid integration — coming soon" with a manual entry fallback. Do not block the financial reporting on Plaid being live.
