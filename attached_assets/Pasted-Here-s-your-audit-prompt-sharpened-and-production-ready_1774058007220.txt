**PHASE 1 — TENANT ONBOARDING & ORG SETUP**

Simulate a brand new organization registering for the first time:

- Trial signup and plan selection flow — does it complete, provision the workspace, and set subscription tier correctly?
- Org settings: pay cycle configuration, payment method capture, staffing email setup, and all platform-required org-level settings — do they save, persist, and sync across sessions?
- Confirm workspace is fully isolated (multi-tenant data separation enforced at every query level)

---

**PHASE 2 — USER MANAGEMENT & END-USER ONBOARDING**

- Org owner invites an end user — does the invite flow complete end-to-end?
- New user accepts invite and completes personal setup: pay rate, pay type, position, and role assignment — do all fields save, persist, and reflect correctly in the org roster and Trinity's awareness?
- Confirm RBAC guards are enforced — users only see and access what their role permits

---

**PHASE 3 — CLIENT SETUP**

- Org adds a new client with full configuration: client pay rate, address, billing schedule, and all relevant settings
- Confirm client data persists, syncs, and is accessible to Trinity and all relevant platform features
- Test that client records are properly scoped to the org workspace

---

**PHASE 4 — TRINITY ONBOARDING & LEARNING**

- Org owner uploads organizational documents for Trinity to scan and ingest — does the document processing pipeline execute, does Trinity learn and retain org context?
- Org owner instructs Trinity via natural language to add clients and configure them — does Trinity execute, confirm progress, and track completion state?
- Trinity's progress tracking must be persistent — if a session ends mid-onboarding, Trinity must resume with full awareness of what was and wasn't completed

---

**PHASE 5 — SCHEDULING, AUTO-FILL & STAFFING INTELLIGENCE**

- Trinity must demonstrate ability to pull from org data (clients, employees, positions, pay types) to generate an auto-schedule
- Simulate open shifts from client demand — does Trinity proactively identify coverage gaps and fill them?
- Simulate an inbound staffing email (calloffs@, incidents@) — does Trinity parse, process, and autonomously respond by filling the shift or escalating?
- Confirm auto-fill logic respects pay rates, positions, availability, and org rules

---

**PHASE 6 — PIPELINES, WORKFLOWS & AUTOMATIONS**

Stress test every active pipeline with real simulated data:

- Hiring pipeline — full flow from applicant to onboarded employee
- All other active pipelines and workflow automations
- Confirm each stage transitions correctly, triggers the right actions, and does not stall or orphan records

---

**PHASE 7 — TRINITY ACTIONS & PAYLOAD GENERATION**

- Execute every registered Trinity action in the action registry

- All outputs must save and correctly append to the associated end user or org record
- Confirm persistence — data must survive session resets and page refreshes
- Confirm sync — data must reflect in real time across all relevant views without stale state

---

**PHASE 8 — SUBSCRIPTION ENFORCEMENT & FEATURE GUARDS**

- Simulate access attempts for features outside the active subscription tier
- Confirm guards block access cleanly with appropriate messaging — no silent failures, no unauthorized data exposure
- Confirm trial limits, feature flags, and role-based restrictions all enforce correctly in tandem

---

**PHASE 9 — PERSISTENCE, SYNC & DATA INTEGRITY (GLOBAL)**

Across every phase above, validate:

- All data written to the database is queryable, accurate, and workspace-scoped
- No orphaned records, null foreign keys, or missing workspace_id references
- All UI state reflects backend state after hard refresh
- Trinity's memory and context of org data is consistent across actions and sessions
