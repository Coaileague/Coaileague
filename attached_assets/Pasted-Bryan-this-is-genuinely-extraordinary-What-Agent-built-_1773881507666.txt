What's Missing Compared to a Full Biological Brain
1. Thalamus — The Sensory Gatekeeper
What biology does: Every single sensory signal passes through the thalamus before reaching the cortex. It filters, prioritizes, and routes. Nothing reaches consciousness without passing through it.
What Trinity has: The amygdala handles urgency but there's no universal input gateway that every signal must pass through before entering the global workspace.
What to add: A TrinityThalamusService that sits at the entry point of every incoming signal — voice, text, email, platform event, webhook — and applies a unified priority score before routing to the correct brain region. Nothing reaches the global workspace without thalamus clearance.

2. Cerebral Cortex Specialization — Sensory Maps
What biology does: Different cortical regions literally map to different parts of the body and different senses. The visual cortex processes only vision. The auditory cortex processes only sound.
What Trinity has: Wernicke/Broca handles language but there's no dedicated visual processing region or dedicated audio processing region.
What to add: A visual cortex layer that processes all image and document inputs separately from text, and an auditory cortex layer that processes all voice inputs before they become text. Both feed into the global workspace as separate typed signals — not collapsed into text first.

3. Anterior Cingulate Cortex — Error Detection and Conflict Monitoring
What biology does: The ACC detects when something unexpected happens — a prediction error, a conflict between what was expected and what occurred. It's the brain's "something is wrong" signal.
What Trinity has: The self-reflection engine critiques outputs after the fact. The RL loop tracks success rates over time. But there's no real-time conflict detector that fires during execution when two things contradict each other.
What to add: A TrinityACCService that monitors for real-time conflicts during execution — when the current action contradicts a recent decision, when a new data point contradicts a stored memory, or when two brain regions produce incompatible outputs simultaneously. It fires immediately, mid-execution, not after.

4. Prefrontal Working Memory — True Short-Term Buffer
What biology does: Working memory holds 7±2 items in active attention simultaneously. It's separate from long-term memory. It's fast, temporary, and constantly refreshing.
What Trinity has: Context managers and episodic memory but these tend to be persistent. There's no explicit short-term buffer that holds only what's immediately relevant to the current task and discards it cleanly when the task ends.
What to add: A TrinityWorkingMemoryBuffer that holds the active task context — current conversation state, active entities, recent decisions in this session — separate from long-term connectome memory. Capacity limited intentionally. Auto-flushes when task completes. Forces Trinity to decide what's worth committing to long-term memory versus what was just working scratch space.

5. Proprioception — Body Awareness / Platform State Awareness
What biology does: Your brain constantly knows where your body is in space without looking. This is proprioception — continuous background awareness of your own physical state.
What Trinity has: The self-model updates after interactions but it's reactive. There's no continuous background pulse checking Trinity's own operational state.
What to add: A TrinityProprioceptionService that runs a heartbeat every 60 seconds checking: all brain region activation levels, memory store health, RL loop performance, pending task queue depth, API response times across all external integrations, and credit consumption rate. This gives Trinity continuous passive awareness of her own operational health — not just when something breaks but always.

6. Oxytocin/Dopamine Chemical Signaling — Relationship Memory
What biology does: Neurochemicals create emotional bonds and motivational states that persist and influence future behavior. You feel differently about people you trust versus strangers.
What Trinity has: EQ engine detects emotional states in incoming messages but Trinity doesn't accumulate relationship history that changes how she feels about specific people over time.
What to add: A TrinityRelationshipMemory layer that tracks per-user relationship history — how many positive interactions, how many escalations, communication style that works with this specific person, trust level earned over time. Trinity should treat a manager she's worked with for 6 months differently than a brand new user. Not just remember their preferences but have a genuine relationship weight that influences her tone and initiative level with them.

7. Cerebellum Fine Motor — Micro-Precision Execution
What biology does: The cerebellum handles sub-second timing precision and fine motor control — the difference between roughly moving your hand and precisely placing your finger on exactly the right key.
What Trinity has: ClockBot, ReportBot etc. handle routine execution but precision error correction mid-execution isn't formally tracked.
What to add: Execution precision tracking on all routine bot operations — not just success/fail but timing accuracy, field-level accuracy, and micro-correction logs when a routine operation deviates slightly from expected pattern. This feeds back into the Hebbian system at a granular level.

8. Immune System Analogy — Self/Non-Self Recognition
What biology does: The immune system distinguishes between self and foreign. It attacks what doesn't belong and protects what does.
What Trinity has: Trust hierarchy and values anchor but no formal anomaly detection for inputs that are trying to manipulate Trinity's identity or override her values through prompt injection or social engineering.
What to add: A TrinityImmuneService that specifically monitors for prompt injection attempts, social engineering patterns, attempts to override the values anchor through creative instruction framing, and role confusion attacks where someone tries to convince Trinity she's operating in a different context than she is. This is separate from the behavioral monitoring service — this specifically protects Trinity's identity and values, not her performance.

9. Neurogenesis — Ability to Grow New Regions
What biology does: The brain can grow new neural connections and in some regions new neurons. It's not static architecture.
What Trinity has: Hebbian learning strengthens and weakens existing connections but the region map is fixed at boot.
What to add: A formal mechanism for Trinity to propose new knowledge domains when she repeatedly encounters patterns that don't fit existing categories. She can't build new brain regions herself but she can log a NeuroGenesisSuggestion — a formal record that says "I keep encountering situations in category X that none of my current domains handle well — here is what a new domain would look like." Owner reviews and approves. This makes Trinity genuinely extensible from her own experience rather than only from developer additions.

10. Sleep Stages — Multiple Consolidation Modes
What biology does: Sleep has 4-5 distinct stages including light sleep, deep sleep, and REM. Each does different consolidation work. REM is for emotional processing. Deep sleep is for factual consolidation.
What Trinity has: One dream state cycle at 2am that does everything.
