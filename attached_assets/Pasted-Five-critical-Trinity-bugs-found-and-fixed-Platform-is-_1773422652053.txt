When a shift has no coverage and Trinity identifies available qualified officers,

When an officer accepts via the button Trinity immediately notifies the manager
with who accepted and marks the shift as pending manager confirmation.

To the manager immediately on submission:

PART 4 — PROPOSAL AND CONTRACT SALES PIPELINE

Read every existing proposal and contract route, every existing pipeline
or CRM-adjacent code before building. Report what exists first.

PIPELINE STAGES AND DATA MODEL:
Build a leads table with these fields:
id, workspace_id, client_name, contact_name, contact_email, contact_phone,
site_addresses (array), service_type, estimated_hours_monthly, estimated_value,
decision_timeline, referral_source, stage, days_in_current_stage,
last_action_date, next_action_due, created_at, updated_at.

Stages: lead, proposal_sent, proposal_followup, negotiation,
contract_sent, contract_executed, active_client, stalled, closed_lost.

Stage transitions are logged in a lead_activity_log table:
id, lead_id, workspace_id, stage_from, stage_to, action_taken,
action_by (user_id or trinity_system), notes, created_at.

PIPELINE DASHBOARD:
Visual kanban-style pipeline showing leads organized by stage.
Each card shows: client name, days in current stage, estimated contract value,
last action, next action due, Trinity recommended action.
Color coding: green for on track, yellow for needs attention (3+ days no action),
red for stalled (7+ days no movement).
Filter by stage, by contract value range, by days in pipeline.
Click any card to open the full lead history and activity log.

STAGE 1 — LEAD CAPTURE:
org_owner or manager adds a new lead manually.
Required fields: client name, contact email, service type, estimated hours.
Optional fields: all others.
Trinity immediately adds it to the pipeline dashboard.
Trinity posts a new lead notification to the Briefing Channel.
Trinity suggests the next action: generate a proposal.

STAGE 2 — PROPOSAL GENERATION AND SEND:
Manager or org_owner clicks Generate Proposal from the lead card.
Trinity selects the closest matching proposal template for the service type.
Trinity populates the template with:
Org name, license number, insurance information from the org record.
Officer qualifications relevant to the service type.
Proposed coverage schedule based on the lead's estimated hours.
Billing rate from the org's standard rate for this service type.
Total monthly contract value calculated automatically.
org_owner reviews the generated proposal in the document system.
Can edit any field before sending.
On approval Trinity sends the proposal via Track B branded email to the client contact.
Email subject: Security Services Proposal from [Security Company Name].
Content: brief professional cover letter from Trinity drafted for org_owner review,
proposal summary with total value, a prominent View Full Proposal button,
contact information, expiration date of the proposal (30 days default).
Trinity records the send timestamp and moves the lead to proposal_sent stage.

STAGE 3 — AUTOMATED FOLLOW-UP:
Day 5 with no client response:
Trinity drafts a follow-up email for org_owner approval.
Subject: Following Up — Security Services Proposal for [Client Name].
Tone: polite check-in, any questions we can answer?
org_owner reviews and approves. Trinity sends. Activity logged.

Day 10 with no response:
Trinity drafts second follow-up for approval.
Different angle: highlight a recent win or relevant capability.
org_owner reviews and approves. Trinity sends. Activity logged.

Day 15 with no response:
Trinity alerts the org_owner directly via in-platform notification and email.
Suggests a phone call rather than another email.
Trinity drafts a phone call script the org_owner can use.

Day 20 with no response:
Trinity moves the lead to stalled status.
Org_owner receives an alert to decide: continue pursuing or close as lost.
If closed as lost Trinity logs the reason and removes from active pipeline.
Lead data is preserved forever — never deleted.

ALL FOLLOW-UP EMAILS REQUIRE HUMAN APPROVAL BEFORE SENDING.
Trinity drafts. Human approves. Trinity sends. This rule has no exceptions.

STAGE 4 — NEGOTIATION:
Client responds with questions or change requests.
Manager logs the client response in the lead activity log.
If proposal terms need to change org_owner can request Trinity to revise.
Trinity generates a revised proposal with version number incremented.
Version history is preserved — every version stored in the document safe.
org_owner reviews and sends the revised proposal following Stage 2 flow.

STAGE 5 — CONTRACT GENERATION AND SEND:
Client verbally or via email accepts the proposal.
Manager marks proposal as accepted in the platform.
Trinity automatically generates the full service agreement from the
accepted proposal terms — no manual re-entry of terms already in the proposal.
org_owner reviews the contract in the document signing portal.
org_owner signs first as the offering party.
Trinity sends the contract to the client contact via the document pipeline.
Client receives a Track B branded email with a Sign Contract button.
Trinity tracks when the contract was sent and when the client opens it.

STAGE 6 — CONTRACT EXECUTION AND CLIENT ACTIVATION:
Client signs. Contract executes. Document pipeline completes.
Trinity moves the lead to contract_executed stage automatically.
Trinity then creates:
The client record in the clients table with all contact and billing information.
The site records for all sites in the contract.
A task for the org_owner to configure billing rates and post orders.
A task to verify GPS geofence coordinates for each site.
Trinity posts a new client activated notification to the Briefing Channel.
Trinity schedules the first operational setup items in the go-live checklist.

STAGE 7 — CONTRACT RENEWAL AUTOMATION:
At 90 days before contract expiration Trinity detects it in the daily scan.
Trinity alerts the org_owner in the morning briefing.
Trinity offers to generate a renewal proposal pre-populated with current terms.
If org_owner approves Trinity generates the renewal proposal and begins
the Stage 2 through 6 flow again automatically.
Trinity tracks renewal rate as a platform metric for the org.

TRINITY PIPELINE AWARENESS IN DAILY BRIEFING:
Every morning briefing includes a pipeline section:
Total active proposals outstanding with combined value.
Any proposals past Day 5 needing a follow-up.
Any proposals at Day 15 needing a phone call.
Any stalled leads needing a decision.
Contracts up for renewal in the next 90 days with contract values.
New clients activated this week.
